# Career Planning

## Definition
Career planning in the Wafflehouse Method is a structured 48-hour introspective process for overcoming feeling lost in tech: take two days off, vividly imagine your ideal 5-year self, distill that vision into 2-3 specific goals, then reverse-engineer a detailed plan using a directed acyclic graph (DAG) of steps from your current state to those goals, accounting for bad luck at every stage.

## Key Aspects
- **Root cause diagnosis**: Feeling lost in tech usually stems from not knowing what you want to be in 5 years, not from lacking technical skills; the goal must come before the tools
- **Technology as tool**: Programming languages, frameworks, and platforms are hammers and nails -- choosing them should follow from the goal, not precede it
- **The Wafflehouse Method**: 48 hours of solo introspection, visualizing your ideal 5-year self in vivid detail, then breaking that vision into concrete 5-year, 3-year, and 1-year milestones
- **DAG of steps**: Create a directed acyclic graph of hundreds of steps from current state to goals; "knot points" (vague or uncertain steps) are resolved through targeted research
- **Plan for bad days**: The plan assumes every day will be a bad day -- account for bad luck consistently rather than hoping for uninterrupted progress
- **Monthly and weekly planning**: Start planning from immediately, not from some hypothetical future date; concrete action starts now
- **Overwhelmingly common**: The source of career anxiety is shared by the majority of CS students and professionals; acknowledging this normalizes the experience

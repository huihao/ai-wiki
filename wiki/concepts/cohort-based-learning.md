# Cohort-Based Learning

## Definition

Cohort-based learning is a peer-driven educational format where a fixed group of learners progresses through a shared experience together, emphasizing self-directed projects, community support, and collaborative exploration rather than instructor-led curricula. The Recurse Center exemplifies this model as a self-directed programming retreat with no teachers or grades.

## Key Aspects

- **Self-Directed**: Participants choose what to learn; no fixed curriculum or instructors
- **Peer Community**: Learning happens through collaboration, code reviews, and discussion with fellow participants
- **Fixed Cohort**: A defined group progresses together over a set duration (e.g., 3 months at Recurse Center)
- **No Grades or Tuition**: Focus on intrinsic motivation and becoming a better programmer rather than credentials
- **Outcomes**: Dedicated time for deep upskilling, portfolio building, network development, open-source contributions
- **Career Impact**: Attendees have transitioned into roles at ML labs and competitive startups (e.g., Mistral AI)
- **Alumni Network**: Long-term community support beyond the cohort period

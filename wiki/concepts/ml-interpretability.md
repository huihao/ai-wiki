# ML Interpretability

## Definition

ML interpretability is the field of making machine learning models understandable to humans, encompassing methods for visualizing, explaining, and guiding model behavior. It bridges the gap between complex computational processes and human comprehension, enabling researchers and practitioners to trust, debug, and improve ML systems.

## Key Aspects

- **ConceptAttention** (arXiv 2025): A method for interpreting generative models by examining how concepts are represented in attention mechanisms
- **Diffusion Explorer** (arXiv 2025): Interactive visualization tool for understanding the internal dynamics of diffusion models during the generation process
- **ManimML** (arXiv 2023): ML visualization animations that make complex neural network processes visually accessible
- **PrefGen** (arXiv 2023): System for guiding generative model behavior through interpretable preference signals
- **Industry applications**: Apple's Responsible AI team works on vision-language model interpretability; Adobe Firefly applies safety interpretability to text-to-image systems
- **Core research interests**: Generative models, multimodal machine learning, and the intersection of ML with human-computer interaction
- **Bridging ML and HCI**: Visualization tools and interactive interfaces are central to making models transparent to non-expert users

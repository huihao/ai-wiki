# Intuition → Empirics → Details

## Definition

Three-step framework for reading research papers efficiently: (1) grasp intuition in three sentences, (2) check empirics via charts and evidence, (3) dive into details only if worth the time. At each step, you can stop — get intuition from 20 papers, invest deeper in only 2 most promising.

## Step Details

### 1. Intuition
- Can you summarize the paper in three sentences in your own words?
- What the paper is doing and why
- Bird's-eye view before diving

### 2. Empirics
- How well does the method work, really?
- Charts are strong proxy for quality: clear visual storytelling vs. incremental benchmark chasing
- Foundational papers have explanatory visuals (GPT-3 scaling laws, Grokking, Chain-of-thought)
- Papers advertise themselves — getting to truth isn't easy

### 3. Details
- Only if you decide it's worth the time
- Code implementation for engineers, definitions/methods for mathematicians
- Derivations can be useful but not always correct (Adam optimizer error, FlashAttention-2 typo)
- People differ most here: engineers prefer code, mathematicians prefer definitions

## Benefits

- Speed: digest 20 papers for intuition, invest deeper in 2
- Research taste: seeing enough papers builds quality sense (Sturgeon's Law: 90% are crap)
- Iterative: can circle back to details later

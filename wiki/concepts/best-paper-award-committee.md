# Best Paper Award Committee

## Definition
The Best Paper Award Committee is the panel of expert reviewers responsible for evaluating and selecting the most impactful research papers at major academic conferences. At NeurIPS 2025, a 14-member committee (including Jacob Andreas, Sander Dieleman, Doina Precup, Yee Whye Teh, and others) selected four best papers and three runner-ups across categories spanning generative modeling, reinforcement learning, attention mechanisms, reasoning, online learning, neural scaling laws, and benchmarking.

## Key Aspects
- **Expert curation**: Committee members are senior researchers with deep expertise across multiple ML subfields, enabling cross-disciplinary evaluation
- **Selection criteria**: Balance of novelty, technical rigor, empirical validation, and potential impact on the field
- **NeurIPS 2025 selections**: Best papers covered gated attention for LLMs, self-supervised RL with 1000-layer networks, diffusion memorization theory, and LLM homogeneity (Artificial Hivemind)
- **Runner-up significance**: The RLVR limitations paper was a runner-up despite being a negative finding, recognizing the importance of rigorous debunking
- **Track structure**: Separate award tracks (main conference, datasets & benchmarks) recognize different contribution types
- **Community signal**: Best paper awards shape research directions, funding priorities, and industry adoption for years following the conference

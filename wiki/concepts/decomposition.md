# Decomposition

## Definition

Decomposition is the architectural skill of breaking down complex problems into smaller, independently understandable and manageable sub-problems, each with clear boundaries and interfaces. It is the foundational thinking pattern behind effective system design and is increasingly recognized as the core competency for AI-assisted coding, surpassing prompt engineering in importance.

## Key Aspects

- The most effective way to leverage AI coding assistants is to decompose large tasks into small, well-scoped units that fit within the model's context window
- Each decomposed unit should have a clear specification: inputs, outputs, constraints, and success criteria
- Decomposition enables parallel execution, independent testing, and easier debugging of complex systems
- In AI coding, good decomposition means the model can reason about each sub-problem without being overwhelmed by the full system's complexity
- Related to the Unix philosophy: programs that do one thing well and compose via interfaces
- Architectural decomposition skills distinguish effective AI-assisted developers from those who struggle with LLM limitations
- Good decomposition also aids code review, team collaboration, and long-term maintainability

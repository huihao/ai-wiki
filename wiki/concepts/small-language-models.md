# Small language models

## Definition

Small language models (SLMs) are lightweight language models typically ranging from 1 million to 10 billion parameters, designed to operate efficiently on resource-constrained devices while retaining core NLP capabilities.

## Key Proponents / Critics

- **Proponents**: Microsoft (Phi family), Google (Gemma), Meta (Llama 3.2), Hugging Face (SmolLM)
- **Practitioners**: John Johnson (comprehensive overview), Ahmad Osman (local LLM guide)

## Evolution

- Early 2024: Phi-2 and Gemma established the sub-3B category as viable.
- 2025: Distilled reasoning models (DeepSeek-R1 1.5B) brought strong reasoning to small scales.
- 2026: QED-Nano demonstrated olympiad-level theorem proving at 4B parameters.

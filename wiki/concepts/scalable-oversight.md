# Scalable Oversight

## Definition

Scalable oversight is a set of techniques and approaches for maintaining human supervision and alignment of AI systems as those systems grow in capability and are deployed at increasingly large scales. It addresses the fundamental challenge that as AI becomes more powerful, the need for reliable oversight grows, yet human attention remains a scarce resource.

## Key Aspects

- Core topic in AI alignment research, featured in advanced LLM courses (e.g., UChicago LLM Course)
- Addresses the gap between what humans can directly supervise and what AI systems can do
- Techniques include debate (AI systems argue opposing positions for human judges), recursive reward modeling, and constitutional AI approaches
- Critical for ensuring intended model behavior as systems become superhuman in specific domains
- Related to concerns about emergent misalignment: capabilities or behaviors that appear suddenly as models scale
- Involves designing evaluation frameworks that remain meaningful as AI capabilities surpass human expertise in narrow areas
- Connects to mechanistic interpretability: understanding internal representations helps design better oversight mechanisms
- Practical implementations include automated red-teaming, constitutional constraints, and multi-agent oversight systems

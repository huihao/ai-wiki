# Clear Domain

## Definition

In the Cynefin framework, a Clear domain is one where cause-and-effect relationships are obvious and self-evident. The correct approach is to sense what is happening and then act based on established best practices or standard operating procedures. Problems in this domain are straightforward and do not require expert analysis.

## Key Aspects

- **Cause-and-Effect Obvious**: Anyone can understand what to do; no specialized expertise needed
- **Apply Established Rules**: Follow standard operating procedures and best practices
- **Sense-Categorize-Respond**: The Cynefin process for clear domains
- **Codified Knowledge**: Instructions can be written down and followed mechanically
- **Example in Coding Agents**: Running a linter, executing a test suite, applying a formatter — tasks with deterministic outcomes
- **Contrast with Complicated**: In clear domains, the approach is always the same; in complicated domains, expert analysis is required

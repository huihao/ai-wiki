# Synthetic Data

## Definition

Synthetic data refers to training data generated by AI models (typically large language models) rather than collected from real-world sources. This approach enables creation of high-quality, curated datasets with specific educational value, particularly useful for training small language models.

## Key Proponents / Critics

- [[microsoft|Microsoft]] — Used extensively for Phi-3 training (TinyStories, CodeTextbook)
- [[ronen-eldan|Ronen Eldan]] — Pioneered LLM-generated children's stories approach
- [[sebastien-bubeck|Sebastien Bubeck]] — Emphasized quality filtering for synthetic data

## Applications

### TinyStories Dataset
- Generated millions of children's stories using LLM
- Used 3,000 words (nouns, verbs, adjectives in equal measure)
- Each story used one noun, one verb, one adjective from list
- Trained 10M parameter models producing fluent, grammatical narratives
- Inspired by Eldan's bedtime reading with his daughter

### CodeTextbook Dataset
- Started with filtered publicly-available data
- Used sophisticated prompting and seeding to capture wider scope
- Repeatedly filtered for quality before synthesis
- LLM processed filtered content over weeks
- Enabled training of more capable SLMs

## Evolution

Traditional approach: Train models on massive raw web data, assuming quantity beats quality.

Synthetic data innovation: LLMs can generate tailored training data with higher educational value than random web content. Key insight: selective, high-quality data enables smaller models to punch above their weight.

Quality control critical: "A lot of care goes into producing these synthetic data, looking over it, making sure it makes sense, filtering it out. We don't take everything that we produce." — Bubeck

Textbook analogy: Approach mimics education—breaking down difficult concepts like a teacher for students. "Because it's reading from textbook-like material, from quality documents that explain things very, very well, you make the task of the language model to read and understand this material much easier."

## Contradictions / Open Questions

**Quality vs. diversity**: Synthetic data may lack the diversity and world knowledge found in massive raw web datasets. Works well for SLMs but may not scale to LLM requirements.

**Verification**: How do we verify that synthetic data produces genuinely better models across diverse benchmarks?

**Domain limitations**: What domains or tasks benefit most from synthetic vs. real-world data?

**Bias propagation**: Does synthetic data generation reinforce existing LLM biases, or does filtering mitigate this?

**Scalability**: Can synthetic data approaches work for training models requiring extensive world knowledge?

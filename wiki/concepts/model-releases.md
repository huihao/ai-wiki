# Model Releases

## Definition

Model releases refer to the ongoing deployment of new AI models and updated versions by major labs, forming a rapidly evolving ecosystem where developers must track capabilities, benchmarks, and pricing across hundreds of competing offerings.

## Key Aspects

- Over 500 models are available across commercial APIs and open-source releases as of April 2026, requiring systematic tracking
- Major providers include OpenAI (GPT series), Anthropic (Claude), Google (Gemini), Meta (Llama), Mistral, DeepSeek, and numerous open-weight labs
- Benchmarks like GPQA (graduate-level reasoning), HumanEval (code generation), and MMLU (multitask understanding) are used to evaluate releases
- Model versioning has become complex: variants include thinking/reasoning modes, high-performance tiers, preview releases, and size-specific editions
- The pace of releases creates evaluation burden -- real-world performance often differs from benchmark scores
- Open-weight models (207 of 341 tracked) provide accessibility advantages, with Kimi K2.6 leading open-weights at intelligence score 54
- The proliferation of model families and variants makes model selection a significant engineering decision

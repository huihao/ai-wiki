# Neural Network Interpretability

## Definition

Neural network interpretability is the field of understanding and explaining how neural networks make decisions, what features they learn, and how their internal representations relate to human-understandable concepts. It combines visualization, feature analysis, and interactive exploration to make ML models more transparent.

## Key Aspects

- Distill publication (2016-2021) pioneered interactive articles combining mathematics, visualizations, code, and discussion to explain neural network behavior
- Feature visualization reveals what individual neurons and layers learn by optimizing inputs to maximally activate specific features
- Activation atlases spatially map millions of activations to reveal how networks organize and relate concepts
- Attention mechanism visualization helps understand how transformers weight different input tokens during processing
- t-SNE visualization reveals clustering patterns in high-dimensional representations, though visualizations can be misleading
- Multimodal neurons in artificial neural networks respond to human-like concepts, suggesting emergent similarity to biological neural organization
- The publication's hiatus in 2021 left a gap in ML communication, though its archive remains a foundational resource for interpretability research

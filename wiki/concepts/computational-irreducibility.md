# Computational Irreducibility

## Definition

A concept from Stephen Wolfram's work stating that for certain computational processes, there is no way to predict the outcome without essentially running the computation step by step. This implies fundamental limits to what trainable systems like neural networks can learn — they can capture patterns and regularities but cannot shortcut truly irreducible computations.

## Key Proponents / Critics

- **Originator:** [[stephen-wolfram]] — introduced in A New Kind of Science (2002)
- **Applicator:** Wolfram applied the concept to argue for fundamental limits of LLMs

## Related Concepts

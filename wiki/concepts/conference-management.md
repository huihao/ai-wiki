# Conference Management

## Definition

Conference management in academic publishing encompasses the organization of peer review, paper selection, scheduling, and proceedings publication for scholarly conferences. It includes the processes of submission handling, reviewer assignment, decision making, and dissemination of accepted research in fields including machine learning and computer science.

## Key Aspects

- **Peer Review Process**: Structured evaluation of submitted papers by domain experts
- **Review Platforms**: OpenReview and similar systems for transparent, open review processes
- **Proceedings Publication**: Accepted papers compiled into published conference proceedings (e.g., PMLR)
- **Paper Selection**: Accept/reject decisions based on reviewer scores and meta-reviews
- **Reproducibility**: Increasing emphasis on code availability,附带材料, and experimental validation
- **Top Venues**: NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR as premier ML conferences with rigorous review standards
- **Community Building**: Conferences as venues for networking, workshops, and birds-of-a-feather sessions

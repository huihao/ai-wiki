# Superintelligence

## Definition

Superintelligence refers to a hypothetical level of artificial intelligence that surpasses human cognitive abilities across virtually all domains, including scientific creativity, social skills, and general wisdom. It is a central concept in AI safety and alignment research, representing both the aspirational endpoint of AI development and a source of existential concern.

## Key Aspects

- Dr. Alan Thompson's LifeArchitect.ai tracks post-2020 AI capabilities as leading indicators, describing the trajectory as moving toward superintelligence with technology already improving daily since 2020
- Thompson's vision: superintelligence will "remove everything negative from life, multiply everything positive," framing it as an overwhelmingly positive development
- The timeline debate divides researchers: some predict rapid advancement (years), others see decades or longer to general superintelligence
- AI capability tracking shows accelerating progress: models now exceed human performance on many benchmarks, but benchmarks may not capture general intelligence
- Safety and alignment research is critical: ensuring superintelligent systems remain beneficial requires solving the control problem before the capability arrives
- Current reasoning models (o1, o3, Claude thinking variants) represent early steps toward more general reasoning, but researchers question whether they develop truly generalizable problem-solving
- The concept drives investment in AI governance, policy frameworks, and international coordination mechanisms

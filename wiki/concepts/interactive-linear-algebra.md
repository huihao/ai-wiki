# Interactive Linear Algebra

## Definition

An interactive online textbook approach to teaching linear algebra that combines algebraic computation with geometric visualization, emphasizing understanding which tools to apply rather than performing calculations that computers can do faster. Created by Dan Margalit and Joseph Rabinoff at Georgia Tech.

## Key Attributes

- **Institution**: Georgia Institute of Technology (Math 1553)
- **Authors**: Dan Margalit, Joseph Rabinoff
- **Structure**: Three sections — solving Ax=b, eigenvalue problems, least-squares
- **Pedagogy**: Over 140 interactive geometric demos, hidden examples/proofs for optional depth
- **Philosophy**: "If Matlab can find the answer faster than you can, then your question is just an algorithm: this is not real problem solving."

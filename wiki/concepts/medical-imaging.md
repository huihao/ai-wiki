# Medical Imaging

## Definition

Medical imaging is the field of applying AI and machine learning techniques to analyze medical images such as X-rays, MRIs, CT scans, and pathology slides for diagnosis, treatment planning, and clinical decision support. AI in medical imaging represents one of the most impactful applications of deep learning in healthcare, with dedicated journals (Radiology - AI), research feeds, and organizational initiatives driving rapid advancement.

## Key Aspects

- Dedicated research infrastructure: journals like JAMA AI, Radiology AI, and Intelligence-Based Medicine focus specifically on AI applications in medical imaging
- PubMed indexes AI-related medical literature using MeSH headings covering machine learning, natural language processing, and medical informatics
- Google AI Health and similar organizational initiatives drive research in automated image analysis and clinical decision support
- Key application areas: automated detection (cancer screening), segmentation (organ delineation), classification (disease grading), and reconstruction (image quality improvement)
- Regulatory pathway: FDA clearance/approval for AI-based medical imaging tools is an active area of policy development
- Data challenges: medical images require expert annotation, handle privacy constraints (HIPAA), and often involve class imbalance
- Integration challenges: AI tools must fit into existing clinical workflows and EHR systems to be adopted in practice

# Frontier AI Models

## Definition

Frontier AI models are the highest-capability large language models available at any given time, representing the current state-of-the-art in AI performance across reasoning, language understanding, coding, and multimodal tasks. These models are developed by leading AI labs (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, DeepSeek, and others) and are the subject of intense benchmarking, comparison, and competition as the AI field rapidly evolves.

## Key Aspects

- **2026 competitive landscape**: Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.7 thinking variants dominate the top positions on LMArena, followed by Google's Gemini 3.1 Pro Preview and OpenAI's GPT-5.4 variants — reflecting the rise of extended reasoning ("thinking") as a key differentiator.
- **Thinking models as a category**: Extended-reasoning variants (Claude thinking, DeepSeek thinking, Kimi thinking) represent a new class of frontier model that performs explicit multi-step reasoning before producing a response, outperforming standard chat models on complex tasks.
- **Rapid proliferation**: Over 300 models are tracked on major leaderboards, with dozens of variants per family (high, thinking, preview, beta, mini) — suggesting rapid iteration cycles and fine-grained capability differentiation.
- **Open vs. proprietary divide**: Top open-weights models (Kimi K2.6, DeepSeek V4 Pro, GLM-5.1) now reach intelligence scores competitive with proprietary frontier models, narrowing the gap that once separated them.
- **Multi-dimensional evaluation**: Frontier models are assessed across intelligence (composite benchmark scores), speed (tokens/second), latency (time to first token), pricing, and context window size — no single metric captures overall capability.
- **Cost-performance spectrum**: Frontier models span a wide pricing range (from ~$0.02/1M tokens for budget models to $10.00/1M for top-tier), enabling cost-performance trade-offs depending on task requirements.

# Deep Research Agent

## Definition

A deep research agent is an AI agent system that autonomously conducts comprehensive, multi-step research by searching, retrieving, synthesizing, and analyzing information from multiple sources over extended periods. These agents combine retrieval-augmented generation with agentic workflows to produce in-depth, well-sourced research reports that go beyond simple question answering.

## Key Aspects

- Operates through iterative research cycles: query analysis, source identification, retrieval, analysis, synthesis, and reflection
- Can autonomously plan research strategies, decide which sources to consult, and adapt its approach based on intermediate findings
- Combines web search, document retrieval, and knowledge base querying to gather comprehensive information
- Uses reflection loops to evaluate the completeness and quality of gathered information, triggering additional retrieval when gaps are found
- Produces structured, well-cited outputs that synthesize findings from multiple sources into coherent narratives
- Frameworks like R2R (Retrieval-to-Research) provide infrastructure for building deep research pipelines with knowledge ingestion and agentic retrieval
- Represents an evolution beyond basic RAG systems, adding autonomous planning and multi-turn research capabilities

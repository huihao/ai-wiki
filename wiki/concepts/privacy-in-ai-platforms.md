# Privacy in AI Platforms

## Definition
Privacy in AI platforms encompasses the considerations, policies, and technical safeguards around how user data, conversations, and personal information are collected, shared, and disclosed when interacting with AI services. It addresses the tension between research utility (using interactions to improve models) and user privacy protection.

## Key Aspects
- AI benchmarking platforms like LMArena disclose that user conversations and personal information may be shared with AI providers and publicly
- Users are explicitly advised not to submit sensitive information they would not want shared publicly
- Continuing platform use typically constitutes acknowledgment and consent to data sharing practices
- Conversations may be disclosed to AI model providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) for research and improvement purposes
- Information may otherwise be publicly disclosed to support community and AI research
- Transparency about data practices is important for research-oriented platforms, but privacy disclosure suggests research utility may be prioritized over user privacy
- Users have limited mechanisms to control how their data is shared or used after submission

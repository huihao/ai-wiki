# Content Preservation

## Definition

Content preservation is the ability to maintain the semantic information and key facts of a document through style transformation, format conversion, or tone adjustment. It is a critical quality criterion in output control design patterns, ensuring that changing how something is said does not alter what is said.

## Key Aspects

- **Information Fidelity**: All key facts, data points, and relationships survive the transformation
- **Semantic Clarity**: Content remains understandable and unambiguous after conversion
- **Style vs. Content Separation**: Successfully changing tone/voice without losing substance
- **Validation Metrics**: Information fidelity scores, style match scores, and semantic clarity ratings
- **Risk Areas**: Neutralization can strip important nuances; style conversion can distort meaning
- **Training Data Quality**: Fine-tuned models must be trained on pairs where content is demonstrably preserved
- **User Trust**: If content is lost or altered during style transfer, the system loses credibility

# Self-Explanation

## Definition

Self-explanation is the cognitive technique of articulating one's reasoning process aloud or in writing to clarify understanding and identify gaps in knowledge. In the context of coding and debugging, it corresponds to the "rubber duck debugging" method, where explaining code line-by-line to an inanimate object forces systematic reasoning and often reveals bugs that casual reading misses.

## Key Aspects

- Rooted in cognitive science research showing that verbalizing reasoning improves comprehension and problem-solving
- Rubber duck debugging: explain your code to a rubber duck (or any non-responsive listener) line by line
- Forces the explainer to slow down and consider each step deliberately, breaking through assumption-based blind spots
- Particularly effective for logical errors where the code "looks right" but produces incorrect results
- The act of translation from internal thought to external language exposes inconsistencies in reasoning
- Applied in pair programming, code reviews, and technical writing as structured self-explanation
- AI coding assistants can serve as "rubber ducks" that respond, providing additional feedback loops
- Research shows self-explanation improves learning outcomes by 20-30% compared to re-reading alone

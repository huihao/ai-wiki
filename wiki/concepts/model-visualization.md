# Model Visualization

## Definition

Model visualization refers to techniques and tools that make the internal workings and outputs of AI models interpretable through interactive visual representations, enabling researchers, practitioners, and the public to understand how models process information and generate results.

## Key Aspects

- Interactive tools like Diffusion Explainer provide step-by-step visual walkthroughs of Stable Diffusion's architecture, demystifying the diffusion process
- AI capability tracking sites (LifeArchitect.ai) maintain comprehensive visualizations comparing model performance across GPT, Gemini, Grok, and other families
- Visual explanations of complex processes (attention maps, gradient flows, latent space traversals) make abstract ML concepts accessible to non-experts
- Component-by-component breakdowns of architectures help practitioners identify where failures occur
- The Diffusion Explainer from Polo Club enables hands-on exploration of denoising steps in generative models
- Model visualization serves both educational purposes (teaching ML concepts) and debugging purposes (diagnosing model behavior in production)

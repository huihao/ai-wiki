# Critical Thinking

## Definition

Systematic evaluation of assumptions, evidence, and reasoning to make well-founded decisions. In software engineering, structured through the "Who, What, Where, When, Why, How" framework, especially important when AI tools can generate plausible but incorrect outputs.

## Key Framework

- **Who**: Verify AI output; accountability lies with humans
- **What**: Define the real problem before rushing to solutions
- **Where**: Context matters — sandbox fixes may break in production
- **When**: Balance quick heuristics vs deep analysis based on urgency
- **Why**: Use "5 Whys" to find root causes, not symptoms
- **How**: Communicate with evidence, seek contradictory data

## Common Biases

- **Confirmation bias**: Favoring information that confirms preconceptions
- **Plunging-in bias**: Rushing to solve before fully understanding the problem
- **Groupthink**: Convergence on same idea silencing dissent

## Techniques

- **Five Whys**: Repeatedly asking "why" to uncover root causes
- **Premortem**: Imagining future failure to surface issues preemptively
- **Seeking falsifying evidence**: Actively looking for data that challenges your hypothesis

# Convolution

## Definition
Convolution is a mathematical operation that combines two functions to produce a third, describing how the shape of one function is modified by the other. In signal processing and machine learning, it is the fundamental operation for filtering, feature extraction, and pattern recognition, most prominently in convolutional neural networks (CNNs).

## Key Aspects
- One-dimensional convolution applies a sliding kernel across a signal, producing a filtered output
- In CNNs, convolutional layers learn spatial feature detectors through trainable kernels
- Sharpened Cosine Similarity has been proposed as an alternative to traditional convolution for certain tasks
- The operation is commutative, associative, and distributive over addition
- In digital signal processing, convolution implements FIR and IIR filters
- Brandon Rohrer's tutorials explain convolution from first principles using intuitive visual examples
- The operation appears across domains: audio processing, image processing, probability (convolving distributions), and time series analysis

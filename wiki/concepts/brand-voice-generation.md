# Brand Voice Generation

## Definition
Brand voice generation is an AI output control technique where a neutral intermediate response is first generated by a language model, and then a fine-tuned model converts that response into the target brand's distinctive style. It is particularly useful when only target-style examples exist (no paired neutral-to-branded data) and formalizing style rules explicitly is impractical.

## Key Aspects
- **Two-stage pipeline**: Stage 1 generates a factually correct but stylistically neutral response; Stage 2 (a fine-tuned model) transforms it to match the brand voice
- **Reverse neutralization**: The name reflects the approach of first generating neutral content and then "re-branding" it, rather than trying to generate branded content directly
- **When to use**: Best when you have examples of the target brand voice but no parallel (neutral, branded) training pairs, and when style rules are too complex or subjective to formalize as prompts
- **Contrast with style transfer**: Direct style transfer via few-shot prompting works when rules are simple; reverse neutralization handles cases where the brand voice is subtle, cultural, or hard to articulate
- **Training requirements**: The Stage 2 model needs fine-tuning on (neutral, branded) pairs, which can be generated by asking the base model to rewrite branded examples into neutral form
- **Part of a design pattern taxonomy**: One of five GenAI output control patterns alongside logits masking, grammar constraints, direct style transfer, and content optimization (DPO)

# Statistics

## Definition

Statistics is the branch of mathematics dealing with the collection, analysis, interpretation, presentation, and organization of data. It provides the theoretical and applied foundations for probabilistic modeling, hypothesis testing, and data-driven inference, serving as a core prerequisite for machine learning, deep learning, and computational science.

## Key Aspects

- **Probability theory**: The mathematical foundation for randomness and uncertainty, including probability spaces, random variables, expectation, variance, and common distributions (Gaussian, Bernoulli, Poisson).
- **Statistical inference**: Methods for drawing conclusions about populations from samples, including point estimation, confidence intervals, maximum likelihood estimation (MLE), and Bayesian inference.
- **Hypothesis testing**: Formal procedures for evaluating claims about population parameters using test statistics, p-values, Type I/II errors, and power analysis.
- **Regression and modeling**: Techniques for modeling relationships between variables, from simple linear regression to generalized linear models (GLMs) and multivariate analysis.
- **Stochastic processes**: Mathematical frameworks for systems evolving randomly over time, including Markov chains, random walks, and Brownian motion — foundational for reinforcement learning and probabilistic graphical models.
- **Applications in CS and ML**: Statistics underpins model evaluation metrics, A/B testing, feature selection, Bayesian optimization, and the theoretical analysis of learning algorithms (bias-variance tradeoff, PAC learning).

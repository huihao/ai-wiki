# Research Transparency

## Definition

Research transparency is the practice of openly sharing methodologies, data, code, and decision-making processes in scientific research to enable verification, replication, and trust in findings. In the context of AI and machine learning, it encompasses publishing model architectures, training procedures, evaluation results, and limitations alongside claims of performance.

## Key Aspects

- Involves sharing complete experimental methodologies, not just favorable results
- Includes publishing code, data, and trained models to enable independent verification
- Critical for AI safety: enables external scrutiny of model capabilities and limitations
- Addresses the replication crisis by making research independently verifiable
- Journals and conferences increasingly require data and code availability for publication
- Pre-registration of studies helps prevent selective reporting of positive results
- Open benchmarks and leaderboards (e.g., LLM diversity evaluation) provide standardized comparison
- Trade-offs exist between transparency and protecting intellectual property or competitive advantages

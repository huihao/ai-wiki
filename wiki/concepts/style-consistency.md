# Style Consistency

## Definition

Style consistency refers to the ability to maintain uniform tone, voice, formatting, and stylistic choices across a body of generated or edited text. In the context of LLMs and AI-assisted writing, it involves techniques for controlling output style to match a target register, persona, or document convention.

## Key Aspects

- Reverse neutralization techniques can transform text between stylistic registers while preserving semantic content
- Style transfer patterns enable converting formal text to casual (or vice versa) using prompt engineering or fine-tuned adapters
- Maintaining consistency across long documents requires explicit style instructions or system prompts that persist across generation turns
- Few-shot examples in prompts are the primary mechanism for establishing and reinforcing target style
- Style consistency is critical for brand voice in marketing content, technical documentation tone, and creative writing voice
- LLMs can inadvertently shift style mid-document due to context window limitations or training data distribution effects
- Evaluation of style consistency remains challenging: no standard benchmark exists for measuring intra-document stylistic uniformity

# Growth Mindset

## Definition

Growth mindset is a psychological concept in learning, emphasizing that abilities can be developed through dedication and hard work. In computer science education, it manifests as the belief that struggling with difficult concepts is part of the learning process, not a sign of inability. Learners with growth mindset persist through challenges, while those with fixed mindset may quit when encountering difficulty.

## Key Attributes

- **Origin**: Carol Dweck's research on motivation and learning
- **Core belief**: Intelligence and ability can be cultivated
- **Contrast with**: Fixed mindset (abilities are innate and unchangeable)
- **Application to CS**: Programming difficulty is intentional; struggle is necessary for mastery

## Application to Learning

- Embrace difficulty as learning signal, not failure indicator
- Persist through challenges (tenacity)
- View errors as information, not judgment
- Accept that mastery takes sustained effort over time
- Build knowledge incrementally through repeated exposure

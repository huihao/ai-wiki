# Confirmation Bias

## Definition
Confirmation bias is the cognitive tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information in a way that confirms one's prior beliefs or values, while giving disproportionately less weight to contradictory evidence. In AI-augmented development, this manifests when engineers accept plausible-sounding LLM outputs without independent verification.

## Key Aspects
- Humans naturally gravitate toward information that validates existing hypotheses
- LLMs amplify confirmation bias because they produce confident, plausible-sounding text regardless of accuracy
- The "plunging-in bias" (jumping to solutions) combines with confirmation bias to skip proper diagnosis
- Countermeasure: actively seek contradictory evidence before accepting conclusions
- NASA research shows cognitive bias worsens under stress and time pressure
- Premortem exercises (imagining a project failed) help surface overlooked risks

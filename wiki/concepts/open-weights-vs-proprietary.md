# Open Weights vs. Proprietary

## Definition

The open weights vs. proprietary distinction describes the spectrum of model accessibility in the LLM ecosystem, where open weights models expose their trained parameters for public use while proprietary models restrict access to API-only interfaces controlled by the model provider.

## Key Aspects

- **Scale of Openness**: Of 341 tracked models on major leaderboards, 207 are open weights -- representing the majority of available models across intelligence, speed, and cost categories
- **Top Open Weights Models**: Kimi K2.6 (score 54), DeepSeek V4 Pro (score 52), and GLM-5.1 (score 51) lead the open weights category, approaching but not yet matching proprietary leaders
- **Intelligence Gap**: Proprietary models (GPT-5.5, Claude Opus 4.7, Gemini 3.1 Pro) still hold the highest intelligence scores (57-60), though the gap continues to narrow
- **Price Advantage of Open Weights**: Open models often provide dramatically lower pricing (Qwen3.5 0.8B at $0.02/1M tokens) compared to proprietary options (Claude Opus 4.7 at $10.00/1M tokens)
- **Customization and Control**: Open weights enable on-premises deployment, fine-tuning, quantization, and privacy-compliant usage, while proprietary models restrict users to vendor APIs and pricing
- **Reasoning Capabilities**: Proprietary models dominate the reasoning category (174 models total), though open weights reasoning models are emerging

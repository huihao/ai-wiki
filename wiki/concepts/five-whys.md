# Five Whys

## Definition
The Five Whys is a root cause analysis technique that involves iteratively asking "why?" (typically five times) to peel away the layers of symptoms and reach the underlying cause of a problem. Originally developed by Sakichi Toyoda for the Toyota Production System, it is now widely applied in software engineering, incident response, and critical thinking.

## Key Aspects
- Each "why" drill-down moves from symptom toward root cause, typically reaching it in 3-7 iterations
- The technique forces disciplined thinking beyond surface-level explanations
- Particularly effective for incidents where the immediate cause is obvious but the systemic cause is not
- Addy Osmani recommends it specifically for AI-augmented development to avoid accepting LLM-suggested fixes without understanding root causes
- Combats "plunging-in bias" by requiring problem understanding before solution generation
- Works best when combined with other critical thinking practices: seeking contradictory evidence, premortems
- Simple to teach and apply, making it accessible for team-wide adoption

# LLM as Judge

## Definition

LLM as Judge is an evaluation methodology where a large language model is used to assess the quality, correctness, or safety of outputs generated by other LLMs or AI systems. This approach replaces or augments human evaluation with automated AI judges, enabling scalable quality assurance in production ML systems.

## Key Aspects

- Used as an output guardrail in generative AI platforms for quality monitoring and hallucination detection
- Techniques include SelfCheckGPT and SAFE (Search-Augmented Factual Evaluation) for detecting hallucinations
- AI judges can assess format validity (JSON, regex), toxicity, brand risk, and factual accuracy
- NeurIPS 2025 research highlighted that current reward models and automated judges are miscalibrated to diverse human preferences
- Part of the broader shift toward eval-driven development, as practiced by engineers like Eugene Yan
- Trade-off exists between reliability and latency; streaming makes output guardrails harder to implement
- Production systems often combine AI judges with human-in-the-loop verification for high-stakes decisions

# AI Benchmarks

## Definition

AI benchmarks are standardized evaluation frameworks used to measure and compare the capabilities of artificial intelligence models across defined tasks and dimensions. They provide objective metrics for tracking model progress, informing selection decisions, and driving competitive improvement across the AI industry.

## Key Aspects

- Dr. Alan D. Thompson's LifeArchitect.ai is considered the "gold standard" for post-2020 AI capability tracking
- Major benchmark platforms include LMArena (crowdsourced comparisons), Epoch AI, and Stanford HAI
- Benchmarks track multiple dimensions: reasoning, coding, math, knowledge, instruction following, and safety
- The landscape includes 300+ models ranked across text generation, with leaders including Claude Opus, Gemini, and GPT-5 variants
- Quarterly retrospectives and deep-dive analyses (e.g., "What's in GPT-5?") provide longitudinal tracking
- Crowdsourced benchmarking from real user interactions complements controlled evaluation suites

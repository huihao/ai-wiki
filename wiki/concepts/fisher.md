# Fisher

## Definition

The Fisher (Fisher's Iris Dataset) is one of the most iconic and widely-used benchmark datasets in machine learning history, introduced by Ronald Fisher in 1936. Hosted at the UC Irvine Machine Learning Repository since 1987, it contains 150 measurements of iris flowers from three species, with four features each (sepal length, sepal width, petal length, petal width), and serves as the canonical入门 dataset for classification algorithms.

## Key Aspects

- The Iris dataset (1936) is one of the earliest ML benchmarks and remains one of the most referenced datasets in ML literature
- Contains 150 samples across three species: Iris setosa, Iris versicolor, Iris virginica — 50 samples per class
- Four numeric features: sepal length, sepal width, petal length, petal width (in centimeters)
- Classically used to demonstrate linear discriminant analysis, k-nearest neighbors, decision trees, and support vector machines
- The UC Irvine ML Repository, which hosts the Fisher dataset, has served the ML community since 1987 and maintains 689 datasets across biology, medicine, finance, and social sciences
- Fisher's original paper ("The Use of Multiple Measurements in Taxonomic Problems") pioneered the use of multivariate statistics for biological classification
- The dataset's enduring popularity stems from its ideal properties for teaching: small enough to visualize completely, clean enough to achieve high accuracy, and structured enough to demonstrate fundamental ML concepts

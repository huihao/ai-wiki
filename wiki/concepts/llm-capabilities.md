# LLM Capabilities

## Definition

LLM capabilities refer to the range of tasks that large language models can perform, from text generation and reasoning to code writing, translation, and multimodal understanding. Dr. Alan D. Thompson's LifeArchitect.ai serves as the authoritative tracker of post-2020 AI model capabilities, providing systematic documentation of how capabilities have evolved across model families (GPT, Gemini, Claude, Grok) and benchmarking progress toward superintelligence.

## Key Aspects

- **Model Capability Tracking**: LifeArchitect.ai maintains dedicated pages for major LLM families (GPT-3.5 through GPT-6, Gemini, Claude, Grok, o1/o3/o4-mini) with regular updates
- **Quarterly Retrospectives**: "The sky is..." series tracking AI progress biannually since mid-2021 (from "on fire" to "delivering" to "supernatural")
- **Capability Categories**: Text generation, reasoning, code writing, multimodal processing, tool use, and agentic behavior
- **Benchmarking Landscape**: Multiple leaderboards (LLM Stats with 296 models) compare quality scores, speed, and cost; Google, Anthropic, and OpenAI lead in quality rankings
- **Scale Progression**: Capabilities have advanced rapidly -- from GPT-3's text completion to GPT-4's multimodal reasoning to o1/o3's deep reasoning chains
- **Open vs. Proprietary**: Open-source models (LLaMA, Mistral, Qwen) increasingly approach proprietary performance; licensing varies
- **Superintelligence Trajectory**: Thompson articulates an optimistic view -- technology improving daily since 2020, with rollout steps behind capability development
- **Industry Adoption**: Microsoft collaborates with Thompson; "The Memo" is consumed by major AI labs and government bodies

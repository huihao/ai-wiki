# Groupthink

## Definition

Groupthink is a psychological phenomenon where the desire for group harmony or conformity results in an irrational or dysfunctional decision-making outcome. In the context of AI-augmented development, groupthink manifests when teams uncritically accept AI-generated outputs, fail to challenge assumptions, or converge on suboptimal solutions because the AI appears authoritative. Addy Osmani identifies it as a key cognitive bias to guard against when working with AI tools.

## Key Aspects

- **AI Amplification**: AI tools can accelerate groupthink by providing confident-sounding outputs that go unchallenged, especially when team members defer to AI rather than exercising independent judgment
- **Confirmation Bias Link**: Groupthink is closely related to confirmation bias — teams may selectively accept AI outputs that confirm existing beliefs while ignoring contradictory evidence
- **Premortem as Antidote**: The premortem exercise (imagining the project has already failed and working backward to identify causes) counteracts groupthink by forcing dissenting perspectives
- **Evidence Over Authority**: Osmani advocates for evidence-based decision making — citing metrics and data rather than deferring to AI confidence or senior opinions
- **Continuous Discipline**: Critical thinking against groupthink requires ongoing practice, not a one-time effort; it must be embedded in team culture

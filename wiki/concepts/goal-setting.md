# Goal Setting (Wafflehouse Method)

## Definition

A personal development framework for tech workers feeling lost, using a structured 48-hour process to go from vague life vision to concrete daily tasks. Uses iterative goal refinement: 5-year → 3-year → 1-year → monthly → weekly → daily, with a directed acyclic graph (DAG) for detailed step planning.

## Process

1. **Day 1 (vision)**: Take 2 days off, go alone to meaningful spot. Imagine yourself 5 years from now at proudest moment. Write everything — feelings, regrets, fears, hopes.
2. **Day 2 at wafflehouse**: Refine vision into 2-3 specific 5-year goals.
3. **Interpolate down**: 5-year → 3-year → 1-year goals.
4. **Build DAG**: Create hundreds of steps from current state to goals, assuming every day is bad.
5. **Unknot knot points**: Research unclear steps; decompose mushed nodes.
6. **Cascade**: Monthly → Weekly → Daily tasks starting NOW.
7. **Weekly review**: Every Sunday, tend to the plan like a garden.

## Key Principles

- Plan for worst case ("sun is shining exactly 0 times for 5 years")
- Goals start NOW, not January 1st
- Don't optimize project management (no Notion dashboards)
- The plan is deeply personal — don't parade it
- Once you have a plan, no one to blame but yourself

# Data Science Roles

## Definition
Data science roles encompass a range of professional positions that combine statistical analysis, machine learning, software engineering, and domain expertise to extract actionable insights from data. The role landscape spans from individual contributor positions (analyst, ML engineer) to staff-level and leadership positions that shape organizational data strategy.

## Key Aspects
- Staff+ Data Scientists focus on organizational impact, mentorship, and technical strategy beyond individual models
- The role requires bridging communication between technical and non-technical stakeholders
- Data science teams in small companies require broader generalist skills than specialized large-company roles
- "Oversimplify communication" is a key skill: translating complex technical findings into actionable business language
- Choosing projects strategically matters as much as technical execution
- ML fundamentals (transformers, CNNs, backprop, regularization) provide the foundation regardless of specialization
- Career progression involves deciding between depth (research scientist) and breadth (staff/principal)

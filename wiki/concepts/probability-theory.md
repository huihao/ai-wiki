# Probability Theory

## Definition
Probability theory is the mathematical framework for quantifying uncertainty and reasoning about random events. It provides the formal foundation for statistics, machine learning, and AI, defining concepts such as probability distributions, conditional probability, independence, expectation, and Markov chains.

## Key Aspects
- Bayes' theorem enables updating beliefs given new evidence, forming the basis of Bayesian inference
- Conditional probability captures how the likelihood of an event changes given known information about related events
- Markov chains model sequences of events where the next state depends only on the current state (memoryless property)
- Probability distributions (Gaussian, Bernoulli, categorical) describe the likelihood of different outcomes
- The law of large numbers ensures that empirical frequencies converge to theoretical probabilities with sufficient samples
- Entropy measures the uncertainty of a distribution; mutual information quantifies shared information between variables
- Foundational to LLMs: next-token prediction is conditional probability estimation, and sampling from distributions generates text

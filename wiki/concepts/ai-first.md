# AI-First

## Definition

An organizational strategy that assumes AI (specifically autonomous agents) is the primary builder and executor, with human roles redesigned around direction, judgment, and exception handling. Contrast with "AI-assisted," where AI tools are added to existing human-centric workflows without structural change. The difference is multiplicative rather than incremental.

## Key Proponents / Critics

- **Practitioners**: [[creao]] (25-person team, 99% AI-written code), OpenAI Engineering
- **Critics**: Those concerned about job displacement, skill atrophy, and over-reliance on probabilistic systems
- **Conceptual support**: OpenAI's harness engineering framework; Stanford Meta-Harness research

## Evolution

- **2023–2024**: "AI-assisted" dominates — teams add ChatGPT/Cursor to existing workflows.
- **2025**: Early AI-first experiments (CREAO, OpenAI Codex team) show 10x+ improvements.
- **2026**: The concept gains name recognition, but most teams remain AI-assisted. Key blocker: building the harness required for safe AI-first operation is hard.
- **Predicted trajectory**: If model capabilities continue improving, one-person companies (Architect + agents) may become common.

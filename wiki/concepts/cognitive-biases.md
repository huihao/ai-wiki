# Cognitive Biases

Cognitive biases are systematic patterns of deviation from rationality in judgment. In software engineering and technical decision-making, the most consequential biases include confirmation bias (favoring information that confirms existing beliefs), plunging-in bias (rushing to solve a problem without fully understanding it), and anchoring bias (over-relying on the first piece of information encountered). Awareness of these biases is essential for critical thinking in AI-augmented development, where the tendency to accept plausible-sounding AI outputs without verification mirrors the same cognitive shortcuts.

## Key Biases in Technical Work
- **Confirmation bias**: seeking evidence that supports your hypothesis while ignoring contradictory data
- **Plunging-in bias**: jumping to solutions before fully understanding the problem
- **Automation bias**: over-trusting AI or automated system outputs
- **Anchoring**: over-weighting initial information when making decisions
- **Groupthink**: suppressing dissenting opinions in team settings

# RL Approach to AI

## Definition

The reinforcement learning (RL) approach to AI is a philosophy championed by Rich Sutton and other pioneers that positions learning through reward signals and environmental interaction as the fundamental path to artificial general intelligence, rather than supervised learning on static datasets or hand-crafted rules.

## Key Aspects

- Advocated by Rich Sutton, one of the founding fathers of modern reinforcement learning
- Core thesis: agents that learn through trial-and-error interaction with environments, receiving reward signals, will ultimately achieve more general and adaptive intelligence
- Distinguished from supervised learning (learning from labeled examples) and unsupervised learning (finding structure in data)
- Key foundational papers cover temporal difference learning, policy gradient methods, actor-critic architectures, and multi-agent RL
- Sutton's "bitter lesson" argument: general methods that leverage computation ultimately outperform methods that try to encode human knowledge
- The approach scales with compute: as computation increases, RL methods improve without requiring human-designed features
- Applications span game playing (AlphaGo), robotics, autonomous systems, recommendation, and language model alignment (RLHF)
- Connections to developmental psychology and how children learn through exploration and feedback

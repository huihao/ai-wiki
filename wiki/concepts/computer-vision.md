# Computer Vision

## Definition

Computer vision is a field of artificial intelligence and deep learning focused on enabling computers to interpret and understand visual information from the world, including images, video, and 3D scenes. It encompasses a wide range of tasks such as image classification, object detection, semantic segmentation, image generation, and visual question answering. Modern computer vision is dominated by deep learning approaches, particularly convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and more recently vision transformers (ViT), which learn hierarchical visual features directly from data.

## Key Proponents / Critics

- Yann LeCun — Developed convolutional neural networks, foundational to modern computer vision

## Evolution

- **1960s-1990s**: Classical computer vision with hand-crafted features (edge detection, SIFT, SURF)
- **1998**: LeNet-5 demonstrates convolutional neural networks for digit recognition
- **2012**: AlexNet wins ImageNet, launching the deep learning era in computer vision
- **2014-2015**: VGGNet, GoogLeNet, and ResNet push network depth and accuracy
- **2017-2020**: Object detection (YOLO, Faster R-CNN) and segmentation (Mask R-CNN) mature
- **2020-2022**: Vision Transformers (ViT) challenge CNN dominance on large datasets
- **2023-2025**: Multimodal vision-language models, video understanding, and 3D reconstruction become major frontiers

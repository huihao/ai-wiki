# Artificial Hivemind

## Definition

Artificial Hivemind is a phenomenon observed in large language models characterized by pronounced homogeneity: intra-model repetition (single models generating similar responses repeatedly) and inter-model homogeneity (different models producing strikingly similar outputs).

## Characteristics

- **Intra-model repetition**: Single model consistently generates similar responses to open-ended queries
- **Inter-model homogeneity**: Different models converge on similar outputs, reducing diversity
- **Mode collapse**: LMs struggle to generate diverse, human-like creative content
- **Long-term risk**: Potential homogenization of human thought through repeated exposure

## Implications

- Raises concerns about AI safety and long-term societal impact
- Limits creative applications requiring diverse outputs
- Affects brainstorming, ideation, and open-ended generation tasks
- Challenges reward models and AI judges on subjective preferences

## Measurement

Studied using [[infinity-chat]] benchmark revealing:
- 70+ models tested show pronounced hivemind effect
- State-of-the-art LMs less calibrated to diverse human preferences
- Reward models and LM judges struggle with idiosyncratic annotator preferences

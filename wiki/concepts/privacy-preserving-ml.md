# Privacy-Preserving ML

## Definition

Privacy-preserving machine learning encompasses techniques enabling model training, inference, and analytics while protecting individual data privacy. Includes federated learning, differential privacy, secure aggregation, and related cryptographic/algorithmic approaches.

## Key Proponents / Critics

- [[google-ai|Google AI]] — Pioneer in federated learning and differential privacy
- Academic researchers in cryptography and ML
- Industry practitioners deploying privacy systems

## Evolution

Privacy-preserving ML emerged from intersection of distributed systems, cryptography, and machine learning. Heritage includes MapReduce for distributed computation, RAPPOR for privacy-preserving analytics. Google articulated federated learning approach in 2016. Key insight: privacy and utility don't have to conflict—can learn from everyone without learning about any one. Applications expanding beyond phones to cars, hospitals, enterprises. Active research areas include measuring memorization, differentially private training, secure aggregation efficiency, and handling heterogeneous devices. Private-by-default ecosystem goal with transparent, verifiable privacy claims.

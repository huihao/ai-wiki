# Cloud-Native Architecture

## Definition

Cloud-native architecture refers to application design patterns optimized for deployment in cloud environments, emphasizing scalability, resilience, and automation. In the context of legacy modernization, converting traditional monolithic applications to cloud-native architectures is a key goal of GenAI-driven transformation efforts.

## Key Aspects

- **Modern Deployment Patterns**: Microservices, containerization, serverless, and orchestrated workloads
- **Legacy Modernization Target**: GenAI converts legacy systems (COBOL, SAS, legacy SQL) to cloud-native architectures
- **Capital Efficiency**: Cloud-native systems reduce operational costs and improve ROI over traditional infrastructure
- **Strategic Agility**: Modern architectures enable faster feature delivery and adaptation to market changes
- **Container Orchestration**: Kubernetes as the standard for managing containerized workloads
- **Empirical Validation**: Case studies from IBM, EY, Virtusa, and Thoughtworks demonstrate effectiveness of AI-driven modernization
- **Scalability**: Horizontal scaling through distributed architecture rather than vertical scaling of monoliths

# On-Policy RL

## Definition

On-policy reinforcement learning is a paradigm where the agent learns from data collected by its current policy (the policy being optimized), as opposed to off-policy methods that learn from data generated by a different or historical policy. This distinction has significant implications for sample efficiency, training stability, and reproducibility.

## Key Aspects

- On-policy methods require fresh data samples from the current policy at each training step, making them less sample-efficient than off-policy approaches
- The requirement for current-policy data creates challenges for reproducibility: even with identical seeds, floating-point non-determinism in GPU operations can produce different trajectories
- Inference determinism is a prerequisite for reproducible on-policy training -- non-deterministic CUDA operations (atomicAdd, cuBLAS heuristics) can cause training divergence
- PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) is the dominant on-policy algorithm for RLHF, using clipped objective functions to prevent destructive policy updates
- On-policy RL is used in instruction tuning and alignment stages where the model's own outputs are evaluated and reinforced
- The approach trades sample efficiency for simplicity and stability -- on-policy methods are generally easier to tune than off-policy alternatives
- GAE (Generalized Advantage Estimation) combines multiple time horizons to reduce variance in on-policy policy gradient estimates

# Premortem

## Definition

A premortem is a structured critical thinking exercise in which a team imagines that a project or decision has already failed and then works backward to identify the most plausible causes. Unlike post-mortems (which analyze actual failures), premortems surface risks and assumptions before they become real problems, making them especially valuable in AI-augmented development where plausible-sounding but incorrect outputs can go unnoticed.

## Key Aspects

- Imagine the project has already failed, then write down why -- surfaces issues that optimism bias otherwise suppresses
- Counteracts confirmation bias and plunging-in bias by forcing teams to consider failure scenarios upfront
- Particularly important when working with LLMs, which produce "plausible-sounding but with no guarantee of being correct" outputs
- NASA research shows cognitive bias increases under stress and time pressure; premortems provide a structured antidote
- Mini-retrospectives on individual decisions sharpen thinking over time when combined with premortem practice
- Communicate findings with evidence and data, not opinions; seek contradictory evidence actively

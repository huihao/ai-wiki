# Pre-trained Models

## Definition
Pre-trained models are machine learning models that have been trained on large datasets to learn general-purpose representations or capabilities, which can then be fine-tuned or used directly for specific downstream tasks. In computer vision, platforms like Roboflow Universe host hundreds of thousands of pre-trained models for tasks such as object detection, instance segmentation, and classification.

## Key Aspects
- Roboflow Universe hosts 250,000+ fine-tuned models alongside 1 billion+ images and 1 million+ datasets
- Leverage transfer learning: knowledge from large-scale training transfers to specific domains
- Common computer vision pre-trained models cover object detection, instance segmentation, and image classification
- Use cases span sports analytics, transportation, safety monitoring, medical imaging, manufacturing, agriculture, and security
- Over 1,000 Roboflow Universe projects have been cited in academic research papers
- Reduce the barrier to entry: practitioners can deploy production systems without training from scratch
- Fine-tuning a pre-trained model on domain-specific data often yields better results than training from scratch with limited data

# Policy Factory

## Definition
A policy factory is a counterproductive platform strategy for GenAI-based coding agents, where an organization enforces standardized rules and top-down controls instead of enabling safe-to-fail experimentation. In complex domains like AI-assisted coding, treating the platform as a rigid policy enforcer stifles the iterative discovery needed to adopt these tools effectively.

## Key Aspects
- Represents the wrong mental model for managing AI coding agent adoption in complex domains
- Implies a top-down control approach with fixed rules, which works for clear or complicated domains but not complex ones
- The Cynefin framework distinguishes four domains: clear, complicated, complex, and chaotic; coding agents fall into the complex domain where cause-effect is only understood retrospectively
- In complex domains, a platform should serve as a learning amplifier rather than a policy factory
- Effective platform strategy should enable safe-to-fail experiments, share emerging patterns, provide guardrails, and accelerate collective learning
- Automated testing and real-time observability replace manual policy as the primary governance mechanism

# Research Taste

## Definition

The ability to distinguish quality research from mediocre work, developed through reading many papers quickly rather than deeply analyzing a few. Sturgeon's Law applies to ML research: 90% of everything is crap. Speed enables seeing enough papers to build intuition for what's good.

## Development

- Read a lot and fast
- Papers don't exist in vacuum — significance comes from web of related ideas
- Spending hours on single paper prevents seeing enough to develop taste
- Speed is safer default: can circle back, can't recover wasted time

## Quality Indicators

- Clear visual storytelling via charts (foundational vs. incremental)
- Explanatory visuals beyond result graphs (e.g., chain-of-thought diagrams)
- Institutions/authors with track record (DeepSeek, Hazy Research)
- Reframing papers connecting threads from different corners

## Where to Find Good Papers

- AlphaXiv: recently popular papers
- Follow authors/institutions with great work track record
- Don't worry too much about sourcing "perfect" papers — ingesting imperfect papers beats waiting
- No ground truth on which papers will become important (PageRank rejected by SIGIR)

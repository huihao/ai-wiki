# AI-Augmented Development

## Definition

AI-augmented development is the practice of integrating AI tools into the software development workflow while maintaining human critical oversight, judgment, and accountability. It treats AI as a powerful but unreliable collaborator whose outputs must be verified, not accepted at face value.

## Key Aspects

- Treat AI output as input from an inexperienced intern — verify everything; accountability lies with humans
- Critical thinking is not optional when AI generates plausible but incorrect outputs
- Define the real problem before rushing to AI-assisted solutions; gather evidence independently
- Apply the "5 Whys" technique to uncover root causes rather than accepting AI's surface-level answers
- Use premortem exercises to surface potential failures before they occur
- Balance quick heuristics (triage) with deep analysis (root cause) depending on stakes
- Communicate with evidence and data, not opinions; seek contradictory evidence actively

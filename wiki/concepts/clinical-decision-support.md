# Clinical Decision Support

## Definition

Clinical Decision Support (CDS) refers to AI-driven systems that assist healthcare professionals in making diagnostic and treatment decisions by synthesizing medical literature, patient data, and evidence-based guidelines. In the context of AI in medicine, CDS represents a key application domain where machine learning and natural language processing help process the overwhelming volume of medical information.

## Key Aspects

- **Evidence Synthesis**: AI systems aggregate findings from journals, trials, and clinical data to support decisions
- **Medical Imaging**: AI in radiology and pathology for diagnostic assistance
- **Literature Mining**: NLP-driven analysis of PubMed, JAMA, and other medical journals
- **Risk Stratification**: Models predicting patient outcomes based on historical data
- **Treatment Recommendation**: Systems suggesting evidence-based interventions for specific conditions
- **Integration with EHR**: Embedding AI insights into electronic health record workflows
- **Regulatory Considerations**: CDS tools must meet FDA and other regulatory standards for clinical use

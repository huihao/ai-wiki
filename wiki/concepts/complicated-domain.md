# Complicated Domain

## Definition

In the Cynefin framework, a Complicated domain is one where cause-and-effect relationships are knowable through expertise and analysis, but are not immediately obvious. Problems require investigation and specialized knowledge to solve, but once understood, solutions can be applied predictably. This is where traditional software engineering practices — learning APIs, debugging, applying well-understood patterns — operate.

## Key Aspects

- **Cause-and-Effect Knowable**: Expertise reveals the right approach, but analysis is required first
- **Expert-Driven**: Requires skilled practitioners who can diagnose and apply solutions
- **Stable Rules**: Once analyzed, the approach can be codified and repeated
- **Traditional Software Fits Here**: Learning frameworks, debugging known issues, applying architectural patterns
- **Contrast with Complex**: In complicated domains, the solution is predictable once understood; in complex domains, it is only visible in retrospect
- **Sense-Analyze-Respond**: The Cynefin process for complicated domains
- **Debugging Culture**: Problems are resolved through systematic investigation

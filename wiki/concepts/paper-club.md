# Paper Club

## Definition

A regular, peer-led reading group where participants pre-read and discuss research papers. The paper club model emphasizes shared accountability, spaced repetition, critical evaluation, and community building. It is a primary learning methodology for practitioners who want to stay current with research without the overhead of formal academic training.

## Format

### Standard Structure
| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Cadence** | Weekly (most effective) or biweekly |
| **Duration** | 1 hour |
| **Pre-reading** | ~1 hour individually before the session |
| **Facilitation** | Volunteer walks through paper in ~45 min |
| **Discussion** | ~15 min free-form Q&A and implications |

### Facilitation Framework
1. **Big Idea** — What is the main point?
2. **Results** — What are the key findings?
3. **Relevance** — Why does this matter? What problem does it solve?
4. **Related Work** — How does it improve on prior work?
5. **Open Questions** — What remains unanswered?

### Paper Selection Heuristics
- **Lindy effect**: Was it relevant 1-3 years ago and still relevant today?
- **Notable artifacts**: Papers for important models, benchmarks, datasets
- **Scaling laws**: Usually contain excellent ablations
- **Code availability**: Papers with code (e.g., FlashAttention) demonstrate seriousness
- **Author reputation**: Good institutions and track records as a filter, not a requirement
- **Word of mouth**: Even "slop" papers can be worth reading to develop critical skills

## Variations

- **Limited run**: Fixed start/end date with a strict syllabus. Lower commitment.
- **Survey paper club**: 5-10 min quick hits on multiple smaller papers in one session.
- **Conference paper club**: IRL meetups during academic conferences.
- **Artifact-producing club**: Members collaborate on slide decks, TLDRs, or blog posts.

## Evolution

- **Traditional**: Academic reading groups in universities, often discipline-specific.
- **2023–2024**: Latent Space popularizes the practitioner-focused paper club model for AI Engineering. The format spreads through Discord communities, corporate learning groups, and remote teams.
- **2025**: Paper clubs become a recognized alternative to conferences and courses for staying current. Tools like Zotero, Elicit, and NotebookLM lower the barrier to pre-reading.

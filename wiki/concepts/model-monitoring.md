# Model Monitoring

## Definition

Model monitoring is the practice of tracking machine learning model behavior, training workflows, and performance metrics in real-time to enable faster iteration, better experiment insights, and improved decision-making during model development. It encompasses experiment tracking, training behavior analysis, and infrastructure observability across the model lifecycle.

## Key Aspects

- **Experiment tracking**: Recording and comparing thousands of training runs to identify optimal configurations and detect anomalies
- **Neptune.ai acquisition by OpenAI**: Signals the growing importance of training infrastructure tools for frontier AI research
- **Training behavior analysis**: Understanding complex model behavior across layers during training, not just final metrics
- **Integration into training stacks**: Monitoring tools integrated directly into development workflows rather than as separate post-hoc analysis
- **Scale of comparison**: Enable comparison of thousands of runs simultaneously to identify patterns and outliers
- **Decision-making support**: Real-time monitoring improves researcher ability to make informed decisions about model architecture, hyperparameters, and training strategies
- **Infrastructure investment**: Major AI labs investing in proprietary training tooling as models become more complex and expensive to train

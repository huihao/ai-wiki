# Deliberate Practice

## Definition
Deliberate practice is a structured, effortful approach to skill development that involves focused repetition of specific challenges at the edge of one's current ability, combined with immediate feedback and systematic improvement. It is distinguished from casual practice or mere experience by its intentionality and emphasis on areas of weakness rather than comfort zones.

## Key Aspects
- Curiosity motivates learning but deliberate practice builds ability; the two serve different purposes
- Playing enough to stay motivated is important, but most time should be spent building hard skills
- Implementing ML models from scratch (not just watching lectures) is the key learning mechanism
- The roadmap from high school math to cutting-edge ML emphasizes deliberate coding practice at each stage
- Classical ML knowledge provides intuition patterns that make modern papers readable
- Undergraduate-level math suffices for implementing new models; advanced math is only needed for theoretical research
- Bottom-up learning (foundations first) builds transferable pattern recognition for new topics

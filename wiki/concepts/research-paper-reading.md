# Research Paper Reading

Research paper reading is a skill that involves efficiently extracting knowledge from academic papers, particularly in ML/AI. The most effective approach follows the Intuition-Empirics-Details framework: first grasp the big picture (intuition), then evaluate the evidence (empirics), and only dive into mathematical details if the paper warrants the investment. Key to developing this skill is reading widely to build "research taste," maintaining a personal system for organizing and rating papers, and using AI tools for comprehension while doing the hard synthesis work yourself.

## Framework: Intuition-Empirics-Details
1. **Intuition**: Can you summarize the paper in 3 sentences? What is it doing and why?
2. **Empirics**: How well does the method actually work? Evaluate the figures and data.
3. **Details**: Only if worth the time. Write code, verify derivations, trace proofs.

## Best Practices
- Read fast and widely to develop taste (Sturgeon's Law: 90% is noise)
- Never let AI write your summaries -- the difficulty of synthesis builds real understanding
- Use a personal database (e.g., Notion) with TLDR, subjective comments, and ratings
- Fill prerequisite knowledge gaps by climbing the concept tree upward
- The tree is not that deep: most concepts connect to a manageable number of foundational ideas

# Real-Time Knowledge Graphs

## Definition

Real-time knowledge graphs are dynamic, continuously updated graph structures that represent entities and relationships in a domain, enabling AI systems to access and reason over the most current information. In agentic RAG systems, they replace or augment static document stores with living knowledge representations that can be queried, updated, and traversed autonomously by reasoning agents.

## Key Aspects

- Static RAG systems hit walls when queries require multi-hop reasoning across multiple knowledge sources
- Real-time knowledge graphs enable dynamic knowledge updates without re-indexing entire document stores
- Agentic systems can autonomously decide when and how to query the knowledge graph based on reasoning needs
- Three architectural patterns leverage knowledge graphs: Reasoning-First with Tool Use, Iterative Retrieval with Reflection, Multi-Agent Orchestration
- Enterprise use cases show dramatic improvements: financial risk analysis time dropped from 4 hours to 12 minutes
- Healthcare diagnostic confidence improved 23% and legal document review speed increased 3x with agentic RAG
- Knowledge graphs support natural multi-hop traversal that flat document retrieval cannot match
- The agentic RAG market is projected to reach $165 billion by 2034

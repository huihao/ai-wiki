# Personal Development

## Definition

Personal development in the technology context is a structured self-improvement process for defining career and life goals, translating vague aspirations into concrete daily actions through systematic planning frameworks, with the Wafflehouse Method providing a concrete 48-hour implementation.

## Key Aspects

- **Vision First**: Start by imagining yourself at your proudest moment five years from now, deliberately removing others' expectations to identify genuinely personal goals
- **48-Hour Sprint**: The Wafflehouse Method allocates two mid-week days for intensive goal refinement -- Day 1 for unstructured exploration of feelings and aspirations, Day 2 for structured refinement into specific 5-year, 3-year, and 1-year goals
- **DAG Planning**: Goals are organized as a directed acyclic graph with hundreds of steps from current state to target, explicitly assuming every day is a bad day to build resilient plans
- **Interpolation Strategy**: Working backward from 5-year goals to 3-year to 1-year to weekly to daily tasks creates concrete next actions rather than abstract aspirations
- **Weekly Review**: Regularly tending to the plan like a garden -- adjusting, pruning, and updating based on progress and changed circumstances
- **Personal Accountability**: Once a plan exists, there is no one to blame but yourself -- a mindset that is both liberating and demanding of consistent follow-through

# AI in Education

## Definition
AI in education encompasses both the appropriate and inappropriate uses of LLMs and AI tools in learning environments, emphasizing that AI should augment—not replace—the struggle that is essential for developing deep understanding and mastery.

## Key Aspects
- **Struggle is essential**: Learning computer science requires sustained effort and tenacity; AI should not be used to bypass the productive struggle phase that builds deep understanding.
- **How NOT to use AI as a student**: Do not copy solutions without understanding, do not use AI to skip struggling, and do not rely on AI for building fundamental skills.
- **How to use AI productively**: Ask for explanations of concepts, request alternative approaches, get feedback on your own solutions, and use AI for debugging hints rather than complete fixes.
- **AI at work**: Productivity acceleration, code review assistance, documentation generation, and refactoring suggestions—where AI amplifies existing skills rather than replacing them.
- **Growth mindset framework**: Effective CS learning follows a structured problem-solving approach: understand the problem, plan, code, reflect, and anticipate edge cases—with AI as a tool at specific stages.
- **30-minute rule**: Struggle for at least 30 minutes before seeking help (human or AI), ensuring you have a mental model of the problem first.
- **AI and the job market**: AI will not replace programmers who adapt; learning to leverage AI while maintaining fundamental understanding remains essential.

# Background Review

## Definition

Background review is a process in self-improving AI agents where past sessions, interactions, or task executions are periodically reviewed to extract reusable skills, identify patterns of success and failure, and distill learnings into persistent knowledge. It serves as a form of reflective learning, allowing agents to improve their performance over time without requiring retraining of the underlying model. Background review typically involves analyzing logs, replaying trajectories, and synthesizing insights into structured memory that can inform future decision-making.

## Key Proponents / Critics

- The concept is emerging in the context of AI agent engineering, particularly for coding agents and autonomous systems
- Practitioners in the AI agent community advocate for background review as essential for long-running agent systems

# EY

## Definition

EY (Ernst & Young) is one of the "Big Four" professional services firms, cited as a key case study provider in research on Generative AI's effectiveness in legacy application modernization. EY's modernization engagements demonstrate how GenAI automates deep-code analysis and converts legacy languages (COBOL, SAS, legacy SQL) to modern, cloud-native architectures, validating the approach's impact on capital efficiency and strategic agility.

## Key Aspects

- EY is among the enterprise consultancies providing empirical case studies on GenAI-driven legacy modernization, alongside IBM, Virtusa, and Thoughtworks
- Their modernization work focuses on converting legacy codebases (COBOL mainframe systems, SAS statistical pipelines, legacy SQL dialects) to contemporary cloud-native architectures
- The article draws on EY's experience to argue that GenAI fundamentally changes the modernization equation: automating what previously required expensive manual rewrites with prolonged timelines
- EY's engagements address efficiency, ROI, and strategic outcomes — not just technical migration, but the business case for AI-driven transformation
- The broader context: traditional modernization methods (manual rewrites, patches, wholesale replacement) consistently yield suboptimal ROI; GenAI presents a new business imperative to disrupt this equation
- EY's role in the research ecosystem: providing consulting-industry evidence that complements academic and vendor-sponsored studies on GenAI effectiveness

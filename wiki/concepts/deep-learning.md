# Deep Learning

## Definition

Deep learning is a subset of machine learning based on artificial neural networks with multiple layers (hence "deep"). These networks automatically learn hierarchical representations of data, with lower layers detecting simple features and higher layers combining them into complex patterns. Deep learning powers modern computer vision, natural language processing, speech recognition, and generative AI.

## Key Proponents / Critics

- **Pioneers (Turing Award 2018)**: Geoffrey Hinton, Yann LeCun, Yoshua Bengio
- **Key architectures**: CNNs (LeCun), RNNs/LSTMs (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber), Transformers (Vaswani et al.)
- **Hardware enablers**: [[nvidia]] GPUs, Google TPUs
- **Critics**: Deep learning is data-hungry, energy-intensive, and lacks systematic reasoning; hybrid neuro-symbolic approaches are proposed

## Evolution

- **1986**: Backpropagation enables training multi-layer networks
- **1998**: LeNet-5 demonstrates practical CNNs
- **2012**: AlexNet wins ImageNet; deep learning revolution begins
- **2014–2017**: GANs, ResNet, Transformers expand capabilities
- **2020–2022**: GPT-3, DALL-E, AlphaFold show scale unlocks emergent abilities
- **2023–2026**: Generative AI, multimodal models, and reasoning networks push deep learning into general-purpose tool territory

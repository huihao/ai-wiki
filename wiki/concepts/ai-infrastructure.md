# AI Infrastructure

## Definition
AI infrastructure encompasses the hardware, software platforms, and tool ecosystems required to develop, deploy, and operate AI systems at scale. It spans from data center build-outs and custom AI chips to cloud-based tool directories and agent platforms that aggregate and govern AI capabilities.

## Key Aspects
- Global competition in AI data center infrastructure, with Europe lagging behind US and China in investment
- Major capital commitments: Applied Digital's $7.5B hyperscaler lease, Tesla's Terafab AI chip project with Intel 14A
- AI tool directories (Creati.ai with 27,484+ tools) reflect the growing ecosystem that needs management infrastructure
- MCP (Model Context Protocol) servers represent the emerging standard for agent-tool integration infrastructure
- Cloud ML services provide managed runtimes, observability (Langfuse + OpenTelemetry), and scaling for production agent deployments

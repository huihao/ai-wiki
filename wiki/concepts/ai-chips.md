# AI Chips

## Definition

AI chips are specialized semiconductor hardware designed to accelerate artificial intelligence workloads, particularly the matrix operations central to neural network training and inference. They represent a critical layer of AI infrastructure, with major tech companies investing billions in custom silicon development.

## Key Aspects

- AI chip development is a major area of competition among tech companies (Tesla's Terafab project with Intel, NVIDIA's H100 series)
- The AI tools ecosystem includes 27,000+ tools, agents, and MCP servers — all ultimately running on AI chip infrastructure
- Europe faces a significant gap in AI data center infrastructure compared to the US and China
- Custom AI chip projects (e.g., Tesla using Intel's 14A manufacturing process) signal vertical integration trends
- Massive capital commitments to AI-specific data centers (e.g., Applied Digital's $7.5B hyperscaler lease) demonstrate infrastructure demand
- The relationship between AI chip capabilities and AI model advancement is tightly coupled — model scale is constrained by available compute

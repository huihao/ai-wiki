# AI Safety

## Definition

AI safety is a multidisciplinary field focused on ensuring that artificial intelligence systems operate reliably, remain aligned with human values, and do not cause unintended harm as they become more capable. It encompasses technical research (alignment, interpretability, robustness), governance (policy, regulation, auditing), and ethical considerations (fairness, bias, privacy). The field has grown in urgency alongside rapid advances in large language models and the prospect of increasingly autonomous AI systems.

## Evolution

- **2008**: Eliezer Yudkowsky publishes early work on the AI alignment problem
- **2015**: [[openai]] founded with an explicit mission to develop safe AGI
- **2016**: OpenAI and DeepMind begin dedicated safety research programs
- **2017**: Asilomar AI Principles drafted, establishing a consensus framework for beneficial AI
- **2022**: [[reinforcement-learning-from-human-feedback]] becomes a standard technique for aligning large language models, demonstrated by ChatGPT
- **2023**: Geoffrey Hinton, Yoshua Bengio, and others issue open letters calling for AI safety regulation
- **2024**: [[constitutional-ai]] and scalable oversight become central research directions at [[anthropic]]
- **2025**: AI safety research expands to cover agentic systems, tool use, and multi-agent coordination risks

# Reactive AI

## Definition
Reactive AI refers to AI systems that respond to specific inputs or stimuli without maintaining internal state, memory, or long-term planning. In contrast to proactive AI, which anticipates needs and acts autonomously, reactive AI operates on a stimulus-response basis, processing each input independently and generating an output based on current context only.

## Key Aspects
- Processes inputs independently without retaining state across interactions
- Operates on a stimulus-response model: input triggers a direct output generation
- Does not plan ahead or anticipate future needs; responds only when prompted
- Traditional chatbots and question-answering systems are classic reactive AI examples
- Reactive systems are simpler to build, debug, and predict, making them suitable for well-defined tasks
- Limited by context window: cannot draw on long-term history or external knowledge without explicit retrieval
- Contrasts with proactive AI, which monitors conditions and takes action without being prompted
- The boundary is blurring as modern systems combine reactive response with proactive monitoring (e.g., AI agents that watch for triggers)

# Bookmark Management

## Definition

Bookmark management is the practice of organizing, storing, and retrieving web page references for later access. At its most basic, bookmark management involves saving URLs to a browser's built-in bookmark system, but modern bookmark management extends far beyond simple URL saving. It encompasses hierarchical folder organization, tagging and categorization, full-text search across saved content, reading progress tracking, cloud synchronization across devices, and export/import capabilities for portability between browsers and tools. Dedicated bookmark management tools and browser extensions provide richer features than native browser bookmarking, including annotation, highlights, dead-link detection, and archival snapshots.

## Evolution

- **1990s**: Web browsers introduce basic bookmark/favorites systems for saving URLs
- **2003**: Del.icio.us popularizes social bookmarking with tagging and sharing
- **2010s**: Read-it-later apps (Pocket, Instapaper) combine bookmarking with content extraction and offline reading
- **2013**: Raindrop.io and similar tools introduce visual bookmark management with rich metadata
- **2020s**: Browser extensions integrate bookmark management with AI-powered categorization and summarization
- **2024+**: AI-enhanced bookmark managers automatically tag, summarize, and suggest related content

# Learning Amplifier

## Definition

A learning amplifier is a platform or system that accelerates an organization's ability to learn from experience, experimentation, and feedback rather than merely enforcing predefined policies. In the Cynefin framework applied to coding agents, the distinction between a learning amplifier and a policy factory determines whether an organization can successfully adopt AI coding agents in complex domains.

## Key Aspects

- In complex domains (where cause-and-effect is only understood in retrospect), platforms must enable safe-to-fail experiments rather than prescribing deterministic workflows
- A learning amplifier shares emerging patterns across the organization, enabling collective learning rather than top-down control
- Contrasted with a policy factory, which attempts to standardize behavior through rigid rules -- inappropriate for complex domains where outcomes are unpredictable
- Provides guardrails that constrain the blast radius of experiments without preventing them
- Supports fast-flow development models that enable rapid iteration and feedback loops
- The platform's value is measured by how quickly the organization learns and adapts, not by how consistently it follows predefined processes

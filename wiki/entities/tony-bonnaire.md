# Tony Bonnaire

## Summary

Tony Bonnaire is a researcher known for contributions to the theory of generative models. He served as the lead author of "Why Diffusion Models Don't Memorize," a NeurIPS 2025 Best Paper Award winner. The paper provided rigorous theoretical analysis using random matrix theory and spectral analysis to identify two timescales in diffusion training: an early generalization phase and a later memorization phase, explaining the practical success of diffusion models in generating novel content without memorizing training data.

## Key Attributes
- Lead author of "Why Diffusion Models Don't Memorize" (NeurIPS 2025 Best Paper Award)
- Research focus includes theoretical aspects of diffusion models and generative modeling
- Applied random matrix theory and spectral analysis to understand diffusion training dynamics
- Work reveals implicit dynamical regularization that prevents memorization

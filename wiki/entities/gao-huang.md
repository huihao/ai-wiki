# Gao Huang

## Summary

Gao Huang is a researcher recognized for contributions to machine learning theory and reasoning. He served as a senior author on "Does RL Really Incentivize Reasoning Capacity in LLMs?", a NeurIPS 2025 Runner-Up paper that delivered a critical negative finding: while RL with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) improves sampling efficiency, it does not expand reasoning capacity beyond what is already present in base models. The work challenges widely accepted assumptions about RL and suggests the need for new RL paradigms.

## Key Attributes
- Senior author of "Does RL Really Incentivize Reasoning Capacity in LLMs?" (NeurIPS 2025 Runner-Up)
- Research areas include deep learning and model reasoning
- Work highlights that base models achieve higher pass@k at large k compared to RL-trained models

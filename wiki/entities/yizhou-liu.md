# Yizhou Liu

## Summary

Yizhou Liu is a researcher recognized for contributions to understanding neural scaling and representation learning in large language models. He served as the lead author of "Superposition Yields Robust Neural Scaling," a NeurIPS 2025 Runner-Up paper. The work identified representation superposition -- where models encode more features than they have dimensions -- as the primary mechanism governing neural scaling laws, showing that under strong superposition, loss scales inversely with model dimension across broad frequency distributions, confirmed in open-source LLMs and Chinchilla scaling laws.

## Key Attributes
- Lead author of "Superposition Yields Robust Neural Scaling" (NeurIPS 2025 Runner-Up)
- Research areas include neural scaling laws and representation learning
- Work demonstrates that geometric overlaps in strong superposition drive power-law scaling
- Validated theoretical findings against open-source LLMs and Chinchilla experiments

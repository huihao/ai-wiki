# Alex Krizhevsky

## Summary
Alex Krizhevsky is a computer scientist best known as the lead author of the AlexNet paper, which won the ImageNet Large Scale Visual Recognition Challenge (ILSVRC) in 2012. His work, alongside Geoffrey Hinton and Ilya Sutskever, demonstrated the power of deep convolutional neural networks on large-scale image classification and is widely credited with catalyzing the deep learning revolution.

## Key Attributes
- **Type**: person
- **Notable for**: Creating AlexNet and winning ImageNet 2012, sparking the modern deep learning era
- **Education**: University of Toronto (studied under Geoffrey Hinton)
- **Other facts**: AlexNet achieved a dramatic reduction in top-5 error rate on ImageNet, proving that GPU-trained deep CNNs could outperform traditional computer vision approaches

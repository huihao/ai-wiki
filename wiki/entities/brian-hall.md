# Brian "Beej Jorgensen" Hall

## Summary

Brian "Beej Jorgensen" Hall is an educator and author known for writing accessible, practical guides on programming and computer science concepts. His guides emphasize hands-on learning, mental model building, and understanding through implementation. He has authored multiple popular guides including Beej's Guide to Network Programming, Beej's Guide to C, and Beej's Guide to Learning Computer Science.

## Key Attributes

- **Known as**: "Beej Jorgensen"
- **Primary work**: Educational guides for programmers
- **Writing style**: Practical, conversational, hands-on
- **Notable guides**:
  - Beej's Guide to Learning Computer Science (v0.11.6)
  - Beej's Guide to Network Programming
  - Beej's Guide to C
- **Teaching philosophy**: Struggle is necessary, tenacity essential, build tapestry of knowledge incrementally

## Educational Philosophy

- Growth mindset: learning requires sustained effort and desire
- Anti-shortcut: opposes using AI to bypass struggling phase
- Practical approach: focus on implementation, not just theory
- Mental models: understand by building correct internal representations
- Rubber duck debugging: explain problems aloud to find solutions

# A.A. Markov

Andrey Andreyevich Markov (1856-1922) was a Russian mathematician who made fundamental contributions to probability theory and is known as the father of Markov chains.

## Key Contributions

**Markov Chains** — Developed the theory of stochastic processes where future states depend only on the current state, not on the sequence of events that preceded it.

**Probability Theory** — Author of "The Calculus of Probabilities" (1900), a foundational text in probability theory that laid the groundwork for stochastic processes and modern machine learning theory.

**Markov Processes** — Introduced the mathematical framework for modeling random systems that evolve over time with memoryless properties.

## Historical Significance

Markov's work in probability theory provided the mathematical foundations for:
- Stochastic processes
- Markov chains and Markov decision processes
- Hidden Markov Models (HMMs)
- Modern probabilistic machine learning

His 1900 book "The Calculus of Probabilities" was recently translated to English for the first time, making this foundational work accessible to modern researchers.

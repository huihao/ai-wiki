# Sepp Hochreiter

## Summary
Sepp Hochreiter is an Austrian computer scientist and researcher known for inventing the Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) network in 1997, together with Jurgen Schmidhuber. LSTMs solved the vanishing gradient problem in recurrent neural networks and became the dominant architecture for sequence modeling tasks such as machine translation, speech recognition, and language modeling for nearly two decades before the transformer era.

## Key Attributes
- **Type**: person
- **Notable for**: Co-inventing LSTM networks, a breakthrough in recurrent neural network architecture
- **Affiliation**: Johannes Kepler University Linz (Austria); also associated with the Austrian AI Research Institute
- **Focus areas**: Recurrent neural networks, sequence modeling, deep learning
- **Other facts**: LSTM was the most widely used deep learning architecture for sequence tasks from the late 1990s through the mid-2010s

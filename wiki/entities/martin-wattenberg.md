# Martin Wattenberg

## Summary

Martin Wattenberg is a visualization expert and researcher known for his contributions to Distill, a machine learning publication that pioneered interactive article formats combining mathematics, visualizations, code, and discussions. His work on t-SNE visualization and feature visualization helped establish new standards for explaining complex ML concepts through interactive media.

## Key Aspects

- Co-founded Distill with Fernanda Viegas, establishing the gold standard for interactive ML explanation from 2016-2021
- Authored influential work on t-SNE visualization, teaching researchers when visualizations are misleading and how to interpret dimensionality reduction results
- Distill pioneered interactive article formats: embedded visualizations, experiments, and working code within academic-style publications
- Publication focused on neural network interpretability, attention mechanisms, feature visualization, and understanding
- Notable Distill contributions include activation atlases, multimodal neurons, and graph neural network explanations
- The publication's hiatus in 2021 left a gap in ML communication, though its archive remains a valuable reference
- Philosophy: "Science is a human activity. When we fail to distill and explain research, we accumulate a kind of debt."

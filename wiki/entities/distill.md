# Distill

## Metadata

- **Type**: Publication / Platform
- **URL**: https://distill.pub/
- **Active**: 2016-2021 (announced hiatus)
- **Founders**: Chris Olah and editorial team

## Description

Distill was a machine learning publication dedicated to clear, interactive explanations of ML concepts with emphasis on interpretability, visualization, and understanding. After five years, the editorial team announced a hiatus in 2021. The platform pioneered interactive article formats combining mathematics, visualizations, code, and discussions.

## Key Features

- Interactive article format with embedded visualizations and experiments
- Focus on interpretability and understanding rather than performance metrics
- Open-access publication with no paywalls
- Editorial emphasis on clarity and visual communication
- Pioneered concepts like activation atlases, feature visualization, and multimodal neurons

## Notable Publications

- Feature Visualization (how neural networks understand images)
- Activation Atlases (mapping learned features)
- Attention Mechanisms (visual guide)
- t-SNE guide (when visualizations mislead)
- GAN Checkerboard Artifacts
- Building Blocks of Interpretability
- Multimodal Neurons
- Graph Neural Networks guide

## Impact

Distill influenced how ML research is communicated, demonstrating that interactive explanations can make complex concepts accessible. The publication's hiatus in 2021 marked a significant shift in the ML communication landscape.

# Deep Learning History

## Summary
Deep learning history spans from the early perceptron models of the 1950s through the deep learning revolution catalyzed by AlexNet in 2012 to the current era of large language models and foundation models. Key milestones include the development of backpropagation, convolutional networks, recurrent architectures, and the transformer. The field has experienced multiple waves of interest, driven by advances in algorithms, data availability, and computing power.

## Key Attributes
- **Type**: concept/field
- **Notable for**: being one of the most transformative technological developments of the 21st century
- **Other facts**: Frank Rosenblatt's perceptron (1958) was an early neural network; the "AI winters" of the 1970s and 1990s saw reduced funding and interest; LeNet-5 (1998) demonstrated practical CNNs; AlexNet (2012) ignited the modern deep learning revolution; the transformer (2017) enabled the current LLM era; key figures include Geoffrey Hinton, Yann LeCun, Yoshua Bengio, and Ian Goodfellow

# A.P. Kiselev

Andrey Petrovich Kiselev (1852-1940) was a legendary Russian mathematics textbook author whose books trained generations of scientists for over 125 years.

## Historical Impact

**Arithmetic Textbook** — Kiselev's Arithmetic was among the most widely used mathematics textbooks in the world for over 125 years, with over **100 million copies** printed. Used in Russia, the USSR, and China.

**Algebra Textbook** — First published in 1888, Kiselev's Algebra served as the official algebra textbook in Russian and Soviet schools for over 70 years, used by tens of millions of students.

## Teaching Philosophy

Kiselev's textbooks were famous for:
- **Crystal clear logical explanations** — Every concept explained with precision
- **Rigorous yet intuitive approach** — Building understanding through systematic progression
- **Focus on true mathematical understanding** — Emphasizing conceptual depth over memorization
- **Define → Prove → Apply → Practice structure** — Every rule derived, not dictated

## Modern Translation

For the **first time ever**, Kiselev's textbooks are now available in **modern English translations**:
- Kiselev's Arithmetic (hardcover, paperback, ebook)
- Kiselev's Algebra Part I (grades 6-8)
- Kiselev's Algebra Part II (grades 8-10)

## Soviet Mathematical Tradition

Kiselev's work exemplified the Soviet-era mathematical education philosophy that produced extraordinary mathematicians, physicists, and engineers through:
- Deep conceptual understanding
- Minimal distractions
- Logical reasoning emphasis
- Strong problem-solving culture

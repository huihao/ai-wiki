# T5

## Summary
T5 (Text-to-Text Transfer Transformer) is a family of encoder-decoder transformer models developed by Google that frames every NLP task as a text-to-text problem — taking text as input and producing text as output. By casting translation, summarization, question answering, classification, and other tasks into a unified text format, T5 demonstrated that a single model architecture and training procedure can achieve state-of-the-art results across a wide range of NLP benchmarks.

## Key Aspects

- **Text-to-text framework**: All tasks are reformulated as text generation — classification becomes "classify: [text]" producing "positive", translation becomes "translate English to German: [text]" producing the translation, enabling a single model to handle diverse tasks.
- **Encoder-decoder architecture**: Uses the full transformer encoder-decoder structure (unlike BERT-only encoder or GPT-only decoder), where the encoder processes the input and the decoder generates the output autoregressively.
- **Pre-training objectives**: T5 is pre-trained with span corruption (masking random contiguous spans of text and training the model to predict them), which proved more effective than token-level masking or language modeling for the text-to-text framework.
- **Scale variants**: T5 comes in multiple sizes — Small (60M), Base (220M), Large (770M), 3B, and 11B parameters — allowing trade-offs between performance and computational cost.
- **C4 dataset**: T5 was pre-trained on the Colossal Clean Crawled Corpus (C4), a cleaned version of Common Crawl containing approximately 750GB of English text.
- **Text encoder role in diffusion models**: T5-XXL (11B) has been widely adopted as the text encoder in text-to-image diffusion models (Stable Diffusion, Imagen), with recent work on T5Gemma (2B) as a more efficient drop-in replacement.

# Jürgen Schmidhuber

German computer scientist and AI researcher, co-developer of the LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) network.

## Summary

Jürgen Schmidhuber is a German-born AI researcher and co-director of the Dalle Molle Institute for Artificial Intelligence Research (IDSIA) in Switzerland. He is best known for co-inventing the LSTM neural network architecture with Sepp Hochreiter in 1997, which solved the vanishing gradient problem and became the dominant RNN architecture for over two decades until Transformers displaced it.

## Key Contributions

- **LSTM (1997)**: Co-invented with Sepp Hochreiter; enabled learning long-term dependencies in sequences, powering speech recognition, machine translation, and text generation for years
- **Curiosity-Driven Learning**: Proposed that creativity and curiosity arise from compressing and predicting environmental inputs (Compression Progress theory)
- **Gödel Machine**: Theoretical self-improving computer program capable of rewriting its own code provably for the better
- **Metalearning**: Work on self-referential systems that improve their own learning algorithms

## Key Attributes

- **Affiliation**: Co-director, Dalle Molle Institute for AI Research (IDSIA), Manno, Switzerland
- **Education**: PhD from Technical University of Munich (1991)
- **Citations**: Over 50,000 (as of 2024)
- **Notable Claim**: Frequently disputes priority credit for various deep learning breakthroughs

## Controversies

- Long-running public disputes with multiple researchers (including Yann LeCun, Geoffrey Hinton) over priority and credit for deep learning innovations
- Known for aggressive self-promotion and claiming broad credit for foundational work in neural networks

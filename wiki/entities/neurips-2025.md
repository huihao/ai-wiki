# NeurIPS 2025

Annual Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems, held December 2025. Featured breakthrough research across generative modeling, reinforcement learning, NLP, learning theory, neural scaling, and benchmarking methodologies.

## Best Paper Awards

Announced November 2025, recognizing seven groundbreaking papers across multiple ML domains.

### Best Papers (4 awards)

1. **Artificial Hivemind** — [[liwei-jiang|Liwei Jiang]], [[yejin-choi|Yejin Choi]] et al.
   - [[infinity-chat|Infinity-Chat]] dataset (26K queries, 31K annotations)
   - Reveals LLM homogenization crisis in open-ended generation
   
2. **Gated Attention** — [[zihan-qiu|Zihan Qiu]], [[junyang-lin|Junyang Lin]] et al.
   - Sigmoid gating improves LLM performance across 30 variants
   - Implemented in [[qwen3-next|Qwen3-Next]] models
   
3. **1000 Layer Networks for Self-Supervised RL** — [[kevin-wang|Kevin Wang]], [[benjamin-eysenbach|Benjamin Eysenbach]] et al.
   - Deep networks (1024 layers) boost self-supervised RL
   
4. **Why Diffusion Models Don't Memorize** — [[tony-bonnaire|Tony Bonnaire]], [[marc-mezard|Marc Mezard]] et al.
   - Identifies generalization vs. memorization timescales

### Runner-Up Papers (3 awards)

1. **Does RL Really Incentivize Reasoning?** — [[yang-yue|Yang Yue]], [[gao-huang|Gao Huang]] et al.
   - Negative finding: RLVR doesn't expand reasoning capacity
   
2. **Optimal Mistake Bounds for Transductive Learning** — [[zachary-chase|Zachary Chase]], [[shay-moran|Shay Moran]] et al.
   - Resolves 30-year-old open problem (Ω(√d) bound)
   
3. **Superposition Yields Robust Neural Scaling** — [[yizhou-liu|Yizhou Liu]], [[jeff-gore|Jeff Gore]] et al.
   - Representation superposition as scaling law mechanism

## Award Committee

14-member committee including Jacob Andreas (MIT), Sander Dieleman (Google DeepMind), Doina Precup (McGill), Yee Whye Teh (Oxford), Xing Xie (Microsoft), and others.

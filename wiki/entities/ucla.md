# UCLA

## Summary

The University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) is a public research university and one of the most prestigious universities in the United States. It is a member of the Association of American Universities and offers a wide range of programs across sciences, engineering, humanities, and social sciences. UCLA is notable in the AI/ML space for its applied linear algebra course, taught by Lieven Vandenberghe and Stephen Boyd, which covers matrix computations with applications to machine learning and data science.

## Key Attributes
- **Type**: public research university
- **Notable for**: applied linear algebra course series, contributions to optimization and signal processing research
- **Other facts**: founded in 1919; located in the Westwood neighborhood of Los Angeles; part of the University of California system

# Mason Wang

## Summary

Mason Wang is a writer who spent three years learning to read research papers efficiently and documented his methodology in "How to Read Research Papers in 5 Minutes." Advocates for speed, developing research taste through volume, and maintaining friction in learning despite AI tools.

## Key Attributes

- **Known for**: Research paper reading methodology
- **Website**: masonjwang.com
- **Bookshelf**: masonjwang.com/bookshelf (paper tracking database)
- **Experience**: Three years treating paper-reading as chore, then as enjoyment

## Contributions

- Three-step reading framework: Intuition → Empirics → Details
- Notion database methodology for paper tracking (TLDR + Two Cents + ratings)
- Saved prompts for AI-assisted paper understanding (but never AI summaries)
- Mentorship from Dhruv Pai (credited for teaching most of the approach)

## Philosophy

- Speed as safer default: circle back later, don't waste time on mediocre papers
- Use AI for understanding, maintain friction for learning
- The tree is not that deep: backfill prerequisites as you go
- Be unapologetically curious: ask precise questions to fill gaps

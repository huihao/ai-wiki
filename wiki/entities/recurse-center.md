# Recurse Center

## Summary

A cohort-based, self-directed programming retreat in New York City. Participants (called "recursees") spend 3 months focused on becoming better programmers through self-directed projects, peer learning, and community support.

## Key Attributes

- **Duration**: 3 months
- **Format**: Self-directed with peer community
- **Location**: New York City (remote option available)
- **Cost**: Free (no tuition)
- **Admission**: Application-based, selective

## Philosophy

- No curriculum or teachers; participants choose what to learn
- Focus on "becoming a better programmer" rather than specific job outcomes
- Emphasis on community, collaboration, and social environment
- Alumni network provides ongoing support

## Outcomes

Based on [[max-mynter-research-engineer-career|Max Mynter's experience]]:
- Provided dedicated time for deep upskilling (Rust, open-source contributions)
- Created CV entry for self-directed projects
- Enabled 15+ PRs merged to highly scrutinized codebases (ruff, uv)
- Network building with like-minded programmers

## Use Cases

- Career pivots (from one programming domain to another)
- Deep skill development (learning new languages/frameworks)
- Portfolio building with substantial projects
- Breaking into competitive roles (ML labs, startups)

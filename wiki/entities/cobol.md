# COBOL

## Summary
COBOL (Common Business-Oriented Language) is one of the oldest high-level programming languages, designed in 1959 for business, finance, and administrative systems. It remains widely used in legacy mainframe systems across banking, insurance, and government sectors. Despite its age, COBOL still runs a significant portion of the world's financial transaction processing infrastructure.

## Key Attributes
- **Type**: concept/language
- **Notable for**: being the longest-lived widely-deployed programming language, still running critical financial systems decades after its creation
- **Other facts**: designed by the Conference on Data Systems Languages (CODASYL) in 1959; designed to be readable by non-programmers with its English-like syntax; Grace Hopper was a key advocate; estimated billions of lines of COBOL code still in production; modernization efforts use AI to translate COBOL to contemporary languages

# Wolfram Language

## Summary
The Wolfram Language is a multi-paradigm programming language developed by Stephen Wolfram and the team at Wolfram Research, and is the underlying language of Mathematica. It is characterized by its vast built-in knowledge base covering thousands of domains (from mathematics and physics to geography and financial data), its symbolic computation capabilities, and its high-level, one-function-call approach to complex operations. The language emphasizes computational knowledge — the idea that algorithms, data, and methods should be built directly into the language itself rather than imported from external libraries.

## Evolution

- **1988**: Mathematica 1.0 released, establishing the symbolic computation platform that would evolve into the Wolfram Language
- **2002**: Stephen Wolfram publishes "A New Kind of Science" exploring cellular automata and computational equivalence
- **2009**: Wolfram Alpha launches as a computational knowledge engine powered by the Wolfram Language
- **2014**: Stephen Wolfram publishes "An Elementary Introduction to the Wolfram Language" to broaden adoption
- **2019**: Wolfram|Alpha Notebook Edition brings the language to educational settings
- **2023-2024**: Wolfram integrates LLM capabilities with the Wolfram Language, combining neural networks with symbolic computation

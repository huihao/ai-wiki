# Zachary Chase

## Summary

Zachary Chase is a researcher known for contributions to learning theory and online learning. He served as the lead author of "Optimal Mistake Bounds for Transductive Online Learning," a NeurIPS 2025 Runner-Up paper. The work resolved a 30-year-old open problem by proving a tight mistake bound of theta(sqrt(d)) for transductive online learning, establishing a quadratic gap versus standard online learning. The proof employed a sophisticated adversary strategy and an innovative "sparse encoding" hypothesis class construction with "Danger Zone Minimization."

## Key Attributes
- Lead author of "Optimal Mistake Bounds for Transductive Online Learning" (NeurIPS 2025 Runner-Up)
- Research areas include online learning theory and computational learning theory
- Resolved a 30-year-old open problem in transductive online learning
- Developed novel proof techniques including sparse encoding and Danger Zone Minimization

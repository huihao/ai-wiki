# Yann LeCun

> Pioneer of convolutional neural networks and deep learning, creator of LeNet-5, Chief AI Scientist at Meta.

## Biography

Yann LeCun is a French-American computer scientist and one of the founding fathers of deep learning. He made groundbreaking contributions to convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and backpropagation algorithms.

## Academic Background

**Current Position**: Chief AI Scientist at Meta (formerly Facebook)
**Professor**: New York University (NYU)
**Affiliations**: Meta FAIR (Fundamental AI Research), NYU Center for Data Science

## Major Contributions

### LeNet-5 (1998)
- **Paper**: "Gradient-Based Learning Applied to Document Recognition"
- **Architecture**: 7-layer CNN for handwritten digit recognition
- **Innovations**:
  - End-to-end learning from raw pixels
  - Sparse connectivity in convolution layers
  - Weighted average pooling
  - RBF-based output layer

- **Commercial Impact**: Bank check reading, ATM systems
- **Historical Significance**: Foundation for modern CNN architectures (AlexNet, VGG, ResNet)

### Deep Learning Foundations
- Convolutional neural network architecture
- Backpropagation optimization
- Gradient-based learning methods
- Efficient training algorithms

### Research Areas
- Computer vision
- Machine learning theory
- Self-supervised learning
- AI architecture design

## Awards & Recognition

- Turing Award (2018, shared with Geoffrey Hinton and Yoshua Bengio)
- IEEE Neural Network Pioneer Award
- Member of National Academy of Engineering

## Key Papers

1. "Gradient-Based Learning Applied to Document Recognition" (1998) - LeNet-5
2. "Deep Learning" (2015) - Nature review article
3. Multiple foundational CNN and backpropagation papers

## Career Timeline

**1988-1996**: AT&T Bell Laboratories
**1996-2003**: AT&T Labs-Research
**2003-Present**: New York University
**2013-Present**: Facebook/Meta FAIR

## Research Philosophy

- Advocates self-supervised learning as path to AI advancement
- Emphasizes efficient architectures over brute-force scaling
- Focuses on understanding fundamental principles

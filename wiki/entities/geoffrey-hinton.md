# Geoffrey Hinton

## Summary
Geoffrey Hinton is a British-Canadian computer scientist and cognitive psychologist widely known as the "godfather of deep learning." A University of Toronto professor and Turing Award winner, his research on backpropagation, Boltzmann machines, and deep neural networks laid the theoretical and practical foundations for modern deep learning. His former students and collaborators, including Alex Krizhevsky and Ilya Sutskever, have gone on to shape major advances in the field.

## Key Attributes
- **Type**: person
- **Notable for**: Foundational work on backpropagation and deep neural networks; often called the "godfather of deep learning"
- **Awards**: Turing Award (2018), shared with Yoshua Bengio and Yann LeCun
- **Affiliation**: University of Toronto; previously at Google Brain (resigned 2023 to speak freely about AI risks)
- **Other facts**: One of the three "godfathers of deep learning"; has increasingly voiced concerns about AI safety and the risks of superintelligence

# Jeff Gore

## Summary

Jeff Gore is a researcher known for contributions to understanding neural network scaling and representation learning. He served as a senior author on "Superposition Yields Robust Neural Scaling," a NeurIPS 2025 Runner-Up paper that identified representation superposition -- where models represent more features than they have dimensions -- as a primary mechanism governing neural scaling laws. The work showed that under strong superposition, loss scales inversely with model dimension, and confirmed these findings in open-source LLMs and Chinchilla scaling laws.

## Key Attributes
- Senior author of "Superposition Yields Robust Neural Scaling" (NeurIPS 2025 Runner-Up)
- Research focus includes theoretical understanding of neural networks and scaling behavior
- Work connects geometric feature overlap in superposition to empirically observed scaling laws

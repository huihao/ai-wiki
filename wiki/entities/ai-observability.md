# AI Observability

## Summary

AI observability is the practice of monitoring, tracing, and debugging AI systems -- particularly LLM-based applications -- throughout their lifecycle. It encompasses tracking token usage, latency, cost, model behavior, agent workflows, and tool invocations. AI observability tools help developers understand how their AI systems perform in production, identify failures, and optimize behavior.

## Key Attributes
- **Type**: engineering practice / tooling category
- **Notable for**: enabling visibility into the otherwise opaque behavior of LLM and agent-based systems
- **Other facts**: key capabilities include distributed tracing of agent workflows, token and cost tracking, latency monitoring, error classification, and evaluation of model outputs; essential for moving AI applications from prototype to production

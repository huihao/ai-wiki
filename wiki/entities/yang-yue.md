# Yang Yue

## Summary

Yang Yue is a researcher known for contributions to understanding the capabilities and limitations of reinforcement learning for large language models. He served as the lead author of "Does RL Really Incentivize Reasoning Capacity in LLMs?", a NeurIPS 2025 Runner-Up paper. The work delivered a critical negative finding: RL with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) improves sampling efficiency but does not expand reasoning capacity beyond what is already present in base models, suggesting the need for fundamentally new RL paradigms.

## Key Attributes
- Lead author of "Does RL Really Incentivize Reasoning Capacity in LLMs?" (NeurIPS 2025 Runner-Up)
- Research areas include reinforcement learning and LLM reasoning
- Work demonstrates that RL narrows exploration while amplifying rewarded trajectories
- Contributed an important negative result challenging widely accepted assumptions about RLVR

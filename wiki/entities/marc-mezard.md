# Marc Mezard

## Summary

Marc Mezard is a prominent physicist and researcher known for foundational contributions to statistical physics, information theory, and their applications to machine learning. He served as a senior author on "Why Diffusion Models Don't Memorize," a NeurIPS 2025 Best Paper Award winner. The paper identified two distinct timescales in diffusion training: an early generalization phase that is dataset-independent, and a later memorization phase that grows linearly with dataset size, revealing implicit dynamical regularization that prevents memorization in overparameterized settings.

## Key Attributes
- Senior author of "Why Diffusion Models Don't Memorize" (NeurIPS 2025 Best Paper Award)
- Renowned physicist with contributions to statistical physics and information theory
- Work applies random matrix theory and spectral analysis to diffusion models
- Known for bridging physics and machine learning theory

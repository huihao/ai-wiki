# All About PhD Applications

## Summary

A comprehensive, popular guide (100K+ visits) to applying to STEM PhD programs, written by a Harvard Neuroscience PhD student and interviewer. Covers the full lifecycle from preparation through decision day, with specific focus on fully-funded science PhD programs.

## Key Takeaways

- **Start early**: Summer before application cycle is the right time to narrow schools and identify PIs. Applications are expensive and time-consuming on top of senior year coursework.
- **Apply to 6–10 schools**: Need 2–3 PIs of interest per school for rotation programs. 14 is overkill.
- **Save money with fee waivers**: Most schools have them — some require FAFSA, others just a short paragraph. Author saved ~$600.
- **Contact PIs before applying**: Helpful for non-rotation programs (must verify space/funding); less critical for rotation-based programs but can provide subjective advantage in interviews. Only ~50% response rate is normal.
- **GREs matter less than you think**: >80th percentile is fine; many programs phasing out. If optional, don't take it.
- **Recommendation letters are critical**: Often as important as the personal statement. Good recs from known PIs carry enormous weight.
- **Statement of Purpose structure**:
  - Why PhD? (short backstory)
  - Previous research experience (bulk of essay)
  - What you want to do in PhD (~25%, goldilocks broad/specific)
  - Why this school/PIs (~25%, customize per school)
  - SoP is a **science story**, not a life story
- **Interview tips**: Only top 5–15% get interviews. Be genuinely enthusiastic. Common questions: tell me about your research, what do you want to work on, PI describes their lab, do you have questions. Key qualities sought: humility, curiosity, ingenuity, resilience, diligence, kindness, integrity.
- **Decision day (April 15)**: Choose for overall happiness and success. Make spreadsheets, then go with your gut.

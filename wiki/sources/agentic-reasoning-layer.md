## Summary

LangChain's conceptual framework for the reasoning capabilities that power agentic systems. Discusses how reasoning is layered within agent architectures and how different reasoning strategies enable more capable autonomous behavior.

## Key Takeaways

- Agentic systems require a dedicated reasoning layer for complex decision-making
- Reasoning strategies include: direct reasoning, chain-of-thought, tree-of-thought, and reflexion
- The reasoning layer interacts with tool use, memory, and planning components
- Different tasks require different reasoning approaches
- Observability into the reasoning process is critical for debugging and improvement

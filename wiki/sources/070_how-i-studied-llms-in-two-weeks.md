# How I Studied LLMs in Two Weeks: A Comprehensive Roadmap

## Summary

A personal account of learning LLM fundamentals in two weeks, structured into three stages: building an LLM from scratch, understanding hallucination, and exploring advanced techniques beyond attention. The author (Hesam Sheikh) shares a curated GitHub repository (ml-retreat) of notes and resources, emphasizing the importance of understanding self-attention deeply and not blindly following any single roadmap.

## Key Takeaways
- Three learning stages: (1) Build an LLM from scratch, (2) Understand hallucination, (3) Advanced techniques
- Self-attention is the hardest but most critical concept - understanding Q, K, V thoroughly is essential
- Hallucination stems from positional bias, exposure bias, and data/training/inference factors
- Advanced techniques: Pause tokens, Infini-attention, RoPE, KV Cache, Mixture of Experts (MoE)
- Key resource: "Build a Large Language Model (From Scratch)" by Sebastian Raschka
- Andrej Karpathy's YouTube playlist is highly recommended for intuitive understanding
- Learning is personal - pick good parts from roadmaps, don't follow any single one blindly
- Identify questions before reading materials to avoid wasting time on irrelevant content

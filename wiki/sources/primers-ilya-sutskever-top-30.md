# Primers • Ilya Sutskever's Top 30

## Metadata

- **Author**: Ilya Sutskever (curator); Aman (annotator)

- **URL**: https://aman.ai/primers/ai/top-30-papers/
- **Access Date**: 2026-04-27

## Summary

A curated folder of 30 foundational papers in deep learning and machine learning, originally shared by Ilya Sutskever with John Carmack with the claim: "If you really learn all of these, you'll know 90% of what matters today." The collection spans CNNs, RNNs, LSTMs, attention mechanisms, scaling laws, and theoretical frameworks like Kolmogorov complexity and MDL. Aman provides detailed annotations and summaries for each paper.

## Key Takeaways

- **Foundational architectures**: AlexNet, ResNet, Deep Residual Learning, Dilated Convolutions, Transformers ("Attention Is All You Need").
- **Sequence modeling**: RNN effectiveness (Karpathy), LSTMs (Olah), RNN regularization (Zaremba et al.), Neural Turing Machines, Pointer Networks.
- **Attention & translation**: Neural Machine Translation by Jointly Learning to Align and Translate (Bahdanau et al.); "Attention Is All You Need" (Vaswani et al.).
- **Scaling & efficiency**: GPipe (pipeline parallelism), Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models (Kaplan et al.), Multi-token Prediction (Gloeckle et al.).
- **Theory & reasoning**: Kolmogorov Complexity (Aaronson, Shen et al.), MDL Principle (Grünwald), Relation Networks, Variational Lossy Autoencoder.
- **Speech & retrieval**: Deep Speech 2 (Baidu), Dense Passage Retrieval (Karpukhin et al.), RAG (Lewis et al.), HyDE (Gao et al.), Zephyr distillation (Tunstall et al.).
- **Long context**: "Lost in the Middle" (Liu et al.)

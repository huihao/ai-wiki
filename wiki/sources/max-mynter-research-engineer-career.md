# Becoming a Research Engineer at a Big LLM Lab

> Source: [Max Mynter Blog](https://www.maxmynter.com/pages/blog/jobhunt)

---

A detailed account of an 18-month strategic journey to land a research engineer position at Mistral, one of the few ML foundation model labs with billion-dollar-plus funding.

## Key Themes

### Tactic vs. Strategy
- **Tactical actions**: Low-effort, high-return in specific situations (LeetCode prep, mock interviews, CV polish)
- **Strategic actions**: High-effort, compound advantages (learning new technologies deeply, building substantial portfolio projects, networking, maintaining personal brand)

### Career Development Timeline
- April 2024: Decided to step up career, initial discussions with manager
- August 2024: First application sent, made it through 6 rounds but didn't get offer (November)
- January 2025: Full-time job search after resigning
- March 2025: Joined Recurse Center (3-month cohort-based programming retreat in NYC)
- June 2025: Tactical phase - focused on applying
- August 2025: Verbal offer from Mistral
- September 2025: Signed contract

### Goal Definition
Specific criteria for ideal role:
1. Career inflecting role
2. Building rare and valuable technical skills (software + ML engineering)
3. Work that is enjoyable
4. Ownership and impact
5. Support from senior peers for growth
6. Path to technical leadership while staying IC
7. Reasonable proximity to important people in enjoyable location

### Application Playbook
1. Batch applications to use lower-stakes interviews as practice
2. Use network for referrals and insider insights
3. Be mindful of referee's time and commit to landing referred roles

### Interview Preparation

**Initial Screen**: "Walk me through your CV" → map experiences to role, give unique spin

**Takehomes**: Async programming challenges (spec + test suite, small ticket in codebase, bug identification)

**Programming Interviews**: LeetCode-style, API/system design coding, real-world challenges
- Preparation: 300 LeetCode problems, NeetCode 150 roadmap, Skiena's Algorithm Design Manual
- Mock interviews with friends and voice LLMs

**System Design**: DDIA (6 months), Alex Xu's System Design Interview, System Design Primer
- YouTube channels: SDFC, ByteByteGo, TRYEXCEPT, Jordan has no life
- Mock interviews using Excalidraw

**Culture Fit**: STAR framework (Situation, Task, Action, Result)

### Networking Strategy
- Twitter/X for building relationships through shared interests
- Offline: cohort-based programs (Recurse Center), meetups, clubs
- Formula: (1) doing interesting things, (2) talking about them, (3) being open and interested

### Career Capital Building
- **LLM experience**: TinyStories-style project, presentation at AI Tinkerers
- **CS fundamentals**: Rust contributions to Ruff and UV (15+ PRs), Recurse Center entry on CV
- **Publications**: Worked with thesis supervisor to rework thesis into peer-reviewed submission

# Interactive Linear Algebra

## Summary

An interactive online textbook for Georgia Tech's Math 1553 course, covering linear algebra with a strong emphasis on the synthesis between algebra and geometry. The text is organized around solving systems of linear equations (algebraically and geometrically), eigenvalue problems, and least-squares approximations, with over 140 embedded interactive demos.

## Key Takeaways

- Linear algebra is best understood both algebraically (writing equations) and geometrically (drawing pictures and visualizing).
- The text is organized into three main sections: solving Ax=b, eigenvalue problems, and least-squares approximations.
- Real-world applications span civil engineering (traffic flow), chemical engineering (reaction balancing), biology (population dynamics via eigenvalues), astronomy (orbit fitting), and computer science (Google's PageRank).
- The "recipes vs. concepts" distinction matters: understanding which computation to ask the computer to perform is more important than performing computations yourself.
- Interactive demos (140+) are central to the pedagogy, allowing students to manipulate points and observe geometric effects of linear algebra operations.

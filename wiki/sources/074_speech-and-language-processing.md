# Speech and Language Processing

## Summary

The online draft of the definitive NLP textbook by Dan Jurafsky and James H. Martin (3rd edition, 2026). Covers the full breadth of natural language processing, computational linguistics, and speech recognition with language models. The January 2026 release includes updated transformer figures, DPO in chapter 9, new ASR/TTS chapters, a restructured LLM chapter, and unicode coverage.

## Key Takeaways
- The standard university textbook for NLP courses worldwide
- 3rd edition covers modern topics: DPO, ASR, TTS, LLMs, transformers
- Updated January 2026 with cleanup, bug fixes, and restructured LLM chapter
- Covers the full NLP pipeline: from text processing to speech to modern language models
- Freely available as online draft - encouraged for classroom use
- Maintained by Stanford (Jurafsky) and Colorado (Martin)

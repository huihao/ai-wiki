# Math For Machine Learning [Resources]

## Summary

A curated collection of math resources for machine learning, born from the author's personal journey transitioning from marketing to ML. Covers pre-algebra through vector calculus, with recommendations for textbooks, video courses, and interactive platforms. The author's key insight: you need math not just to pass courses but to truly understand gradient descent and optimization at a deep level.

## Key Takeaways
- Recommended book: "Mathematics for Machine Learning" for comprehensive coverage
- Linear algebra is the backbone of ML; use David Lay's textbook + interactive websites
- Calculus is essential for understanding gradient descent; 3Blue1Brown Essence of Calculus is great for intuition
- MathAcademy.com is highly recommended as an AI-powered, gamified math learning platform
- The author's journey: perceptron -> Adaline -> gradient descent -> realized vector calculus was needed
- Prof. Leonard's lectures sync perfectly with Ron Larson's Calculus textbook
- Practice exercises are essential - watching videos creates false sense of confidence
- Probability and statistics are essential for making informed ML decisions

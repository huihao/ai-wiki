# How to Stop Feeling Lost in Tech: The Wafflehouse Method

## Summary

A personal development framework for feeling lost in tech, born from repeated conversations with stressed CS students. The "Wafflehouse Method" is a structured 48-hour introspective process: take two days off, vividly imagine your ideal 5-year self, refine that into specific goals, then reverse-engineer a detailed plan using a directed acyclic graph (DAG) of steps from current state to goals.

## Key Takeaways
- Feeling lost in tech is extremely common; the root cause is often not knowing what you want to be in 5 years
- Technology is just a set of tools (like hammers and nails) - the goal should come first, tech second
- The Wafflehouse Method: 48 hours of solo introspection, visualizing your ideal 5-year self
- Break the vision into 2-3 specific 5-year goals, then interpolate to 3-year and 1-year milestones
- Create a DAG of hundreds of steps from current state to goals, then unknot vague "knot points" with research
- Plan monthly and weekly tasks starting immediately, not from some future date
- Plan assumes "every day will be a bad day" - account for bad luck consistently

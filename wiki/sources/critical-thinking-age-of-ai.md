# Critical Thinking During the Age of AI

## Summary

Essay applying the "Who, What, Where, When, Why, How" framework to critical thinking for software engineers in an AI-augmented world. Argues that critical thinking is not optional but essential when AI can generate plausible but incorrect outputs.

## Key Takeaways

- **Who**: Treat AI output as input from an inexperienced intern — verify everything. Accountability lies with humans.
- **What**: Define the real problem before rushing to solutions. Gather evidence; don't accept plausible-sounding AI answers as facts.
- **Where**: Context is king. A fix in sandbox may break in production. Consider where side effects might occur.
- **When**: Balance quick heuristics (triage) vs deep analysis (root cause). Acknowledge error risk under time pressure.
- **Why**: Use the "5 Whys" technique to uncover root causes. Avoid confirmation bias and plunging-in bias.
- **How**: Communicate with evidence and data, not opinions. Seek contradictory evidence. Use premortem exercises.
- LLMs are "plausible-sounding but with no guarantee of being correct" — a critical flaw with real consequences.
- Critical thinking protects against quick-fix allure and saves time by catching issues upstream.

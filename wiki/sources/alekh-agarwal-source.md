## Summary

Personal academic homepage of Alekh Agarwal, a researcher in reinforcement learning theory, contextual bandits, and statistical learning. The page catalogs his extensive publication record spanning RL theory, policy gradient methods, active learning, offline RL, and fair machine learning, alongside teaching history and professional service roles.

## Key Takeaways

- Co-authored an **RL theory monograph** with Nan Jiang (UIUC) and Sham Kakade (UW), based on graduate courses.
- Landmark paper: *"On the Theory of Policy Gradient Methods: Optimality, Approximation, and Distribution Shift"* (JMLR 2020) with Sham Kakade, Jason Lee, and Gaurav Mahajan.
- **Outstanding paper award** at ICML 2022 for *"Adversarially Trained Actor Critic for Offline Reinforcement Learning"* with Ching-An Cheng, Tengyang Xie, and Nan Jiang.
- **Best paper award** at NIPS 2015 for *"Fast Convergence of Regularized Learning in Games"* with Vasilis Syrgkanis, Haipeng Luo, and Rob Schapire.
- Served as **Program Chair for NeurIPS 2022**.
- Long-standing collaboration with Microsoft Research colleagues including John Langford, Miroslav Dudík, and Akshay Krishnamurthy.
- Teaching experience at University of Washington (CSE 599: RL and Bandits) and Columbia University.

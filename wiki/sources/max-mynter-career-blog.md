## Summary

Max Mynter's detailed account of his 18-month journey to become a research engineer at Mistral AI. Covers tactical vs. strategic actions, networking, portfolio projects, interview preparation, and mental resilience.

## Key Takeaways

- Tactical actions (LeetCode, mock interviews) have high immediate return; strategic actions (deep learning, OSS contributions, network building) compound over time.
- Joined Recurse Center for 3 months of dedicated upskilling: learned Rust, contributed 15+ PRs to ruff/uv, and reworked a thesis into a publication.
- Built career capital by closing skill gaps: LLM project (style transfer), Rust/systems programming, and research publication.
- Application playbook: batch applications, use lower-stakes interviews as practice, secure multiple offers for leverage.
- Networking via "weak ties" and sharing work publicly (Twitter, blog posts, talks) was instrumental.
- Interview types encountered: LeetCode, API/system design, real-world challenges, system design, culture fit, quizzes, hiring manager/CTO, reference checks.

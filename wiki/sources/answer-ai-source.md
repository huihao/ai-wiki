## Summary

Answer.AI is a new kind of AI R&D lab founded by [[jeremy-howard]] and [[rachel-thomas]] (co-founders of [[nbdev-fastai|fast.ai]]). It creates practical end-user products based on foundational research breakthroughs. The lab emphasizes open-source work and practical applicability over pure research.

## Key Takeaways

- **Mission**: Bridge foundational research and practical end-user products.
- **Notable projects**:
  - **ModernBERT** (Dec 2024) — State-of-the-art encoder-only model, 8192 sequence length, faster than BERT, better downstream performance. Positioned as a replacement for BERT in production systems.
  - **rerankers** (Sep 2024) — Lightweight Python library unifying ranking methods for retrieval systems.
  - **/llms.txt** (Sep 2024) — Proposal for a standard to help LLMs use websites (similar to robots.txt but for LLMs).
  - **JaColBERTv2.5** (Aug 2024) — Optimized retrieval training for lower-resource languages (Japanese).
  - **Claudette** (Jun 2024) — A new kind of literate programming tool.
  - **flexicache** (Jun 2025) — Exploration of caching strategies.
  - **Solveit** (Oct 2025) — Positioned as "the antidote to AI fatigue."
- **Philosophy**: Practical, open-source, research-informed product development.

## Raw Notes

- Answer.AI represents a continuation of the fast.ai philosophy: practical AI, open source, minimal prerequisites.
- ModernBERT is significant because encoder-only models (BERT class) are still widely used in production for classification and retrieval, despite the generative AI hype.
- The /llms.txt proposal is an interesting standardization effort that could improve how LLMs interact with websites.

# ML Systems Textbook

## Summary
"Machine Learning Systems: Principles and Practices of Engineering Artificially Intelligent Systems" is a comprehensive textbook published by MIT Press (2026), developed from CS249r at Harvard University. It bridges the gap between ML theory and production engineering, covering data engineering, model optimization, hardware-aware training, and inference acceleration -- the systems perspective that most ML education misses.

## Key Takeaways
- Most students learn to train models but not how to build the systems that make them work in production
- The bottleneck in AI progress is shifting from algorithms to engineers who can build efficient, scalable systems
- ML systems thinking encompasses the full stack: data engineering, model optimization, hardware awareness, and inference
- The textbook was collaboratively developed through real student contributions at Harvard (Fall 2023)
- 2026 goal: help 100,000 students learn ML systems; targeting 1 million learners by 2030
- The EDGE AI Foundation sponsors the project, matching GitHub stars with funding
- Content bridges the gap between "seeing the trees" (individual components) and "seeing the forest" (holistic architecture)

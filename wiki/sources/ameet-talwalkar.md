## Summary

Academic research homepage of Ameet Talwalkar, CMU professor with extensive publications spanning AutoML, federated learning, interpretable ML, neural architecture search, and hyperparameter optimization.

## Key Takeaways

- **Professor at CMU** with broad research interests in ML systems and theory.
- **Foundational contributions**:
  - **Hyperband** (JMLR 2018, ICLR 2017 Best Student Presentation): Novel bandit-based hyperparameter optimization with L. Li, K. Jamieson, G. DeSalvo, A. Rostamizadeh
  - **MLlib** (JMLR 2016): Machine learning library for Apache Spark
  - **Foundations of Machine Learning** textbook (MIT Press, 2012/2018): With M. Mohri and A. Rostamizadeh
  - **Federated Learning**: Field guide and FedDANE with Virginia Smith; LEAF benchmark
- **Recent research directions** (2024–2025):
  - Time series foundation models (observability perspective)
  - LLM-driven PDE solver generation (CodePDE)
  - Agents that reason by scaling test-time interaction
  - ML-based combinatorial optimization solvers
  - Interactive feedback for code LLMs
  - Specialized foundation models vs. supervised baselines
  - Web agents (ScribeAgent)
  - LLM pruning (structured pruning with forward passes only)
- **Notable co-authors**: Virginia Smith, Maria-Florina Balcan, Zico Kolter, Geoffrey Hinton (indirect), Graham Neubig
- **Service**: MLSys co-founder, extensive conference reviewing/organizing

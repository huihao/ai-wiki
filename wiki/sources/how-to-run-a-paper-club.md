## Summary

A practical guide to running a paper club based on Latent Space's experience hosting 100+ papers over 18 months. Covers mechanics (scheduling, pre-reading, facilitating), paper selection criteria, tooling, and variations on the format. Also announces Latent Space Paper Club LIVE at NeurIPS 2024.

## Key Takeaways

- **Why paper clubs**: 4 core benefits — shared accountability, spaced repetition learning, staying close to "ground truth" (vs. social media shallow takes), developing critical thinking about flaws and quality in research.
- **Mechanics**:
  - **Schedule**: Weekly, fixed time (e.g., Wednesday lunch), 1 hour.
  - **Pre-reading**: Essential. Skipping pre-reading reduces discussion value by ~80%. ~1 hour using Zotero.
  - **Facilitating**: Volunteer walks through the paper (motivation, related work, methodology, results) in ~45 min, followed by ~15 min free-form discussion.
  - **Volunteers**: Ask for next week's volunteer *during* the current session to establish routine.
- **Paper selection criteria**:
  - Will it be relevant in 1-2 years? (Lindy effect)
  - Notable model papers (GPT, Llama, OLMo)
  - Notable benchmarks (SWE-Bench)
  - Notable datasets (FineWeb)
  - Scaling laws papers (great ablations)
  - Survey papers
  - Papers with code (demonstrates seriousness, e.g., FlashAttention)
  - Reputable authors (though not strictly credentialist)
  - Good word of mouth (even "slop" papers can be worth reading to criticize substantively)
- **Tools**: Zotero (paper management), Luma (event scheduling), Zoom (hosting), Slido (voting/backlog), EmergentMind (paper discovery).
- **Variations**:
  - **Limited run**: Fixed start/end date with strict syllabus.
  - **Produce artifacts**: Slide decks, collaborative TLDRs, writeups.
  - **Survey paper club**: 5-10 min quick hits on papers too small for full discussion.
  - **Conference paper clubs**: IRL meetups during conferences.
- **Community aspect**: "Paper" is only 50% of "paper club." The social element — friendships, job referrals, funding connections — is equally important.
- **ROI claim**: ~2 hours/week (1 pre-read + 1 discussion) × 50 weeks = ~100 hours/year. This places you in the top 5% of AI engineers and top 0.1% of the world in AI knowledge.

## Raw Notes

- The paper club model is designed for working practitioners, not academics. Focus is on applicability and critical evaluation, not theoretical depth.
- The "grad student slop vs. kino" distinction is a useful heuristic for filtering noise in the research literature.
- Attaching to an existing community (Discord, podcast audience) significantly helps with recruitment and retention.
- Recording facilitation but not Q&A is an interesting design choice that incentivizes live attendance while preserving some knowledge.

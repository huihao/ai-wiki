## Summary

Chris Richardson applies the Cynefin framework to argue that using GenAI coding agents is a *complex* domain, not clear or complicated. This means organizations cannot standardize a "one true way" — they must adopt safe experimentation, rapid feedback, and continuous adaptation.

## Key Takeaways

- Cynefin framework: clear (obvious cause-effect), complicated (expert analysis needed), complex (understood only in retrospect), chaotic (no order).
- Software development as a whole is complex; individual tasks may be clear or complicated.
- Developer tools are typically clear/complicated: stable cause-effect, learnable through analysis.
- Using LLM-based coding agents is complex because prompt-outcome relationships cannot be fully predicted in advance.
- Probabilistic nature of LLMs: same prompt can yield different outputs depending on sampling.
- Organizations must: shift to fast feedback, replace manual policy with automated testing/observability, treat platform as learning amplifier not policy factory.
- The "one true way" is neither fully knowable nor stable; practices evolve as models and tooling change.

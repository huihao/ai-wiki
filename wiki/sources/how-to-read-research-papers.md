# How to Read Research Papers, in 5 Minutes

## Summary
Mason Wang shares three principles for efficiently reading ML research papers: (1) follow the Intuition-Empirics-Details framework in that order, stopping whenever the paper no longer seems worth the investment; (2) ruthlessly optimize your personal process for reading speed, developing research taste, and finding quality sources; and (3) use AI tools for understanding but do the hard thinking (summarizing, critiquing) yourself to build real comprehension.

## Key Takeaways
- Follow the Intuition-Empirics-Details framework: grasp the big picture, check the evidence, dive into details only if warranted
- How cleanly a paper tells its story through figures is a strong proxy for paper quality
- Speed is the safer default when reading; you can always circle back to important papers
- Sturgeon's Law applies heavily to ML research: 90% of everything is crap, so read widely and fast
- Never let AI write your summaries or personal comments -- the difficulty of synthesis is the point
- Many papers are hard to understand because you're missing prerequisite ideas; climb the tree upward to fill gaps
- Even influential papers contain errors (Adam optimizer's convergence proof, FlashAttention-2 derivation)
- AlphaXiv is a good starting point for finding recently popular papers
- DeepSeek and Hazy Research (Chris Re's lab) consistently produce high-quality work
- Join reading groups or find mentors: learning is faster and more fun with others

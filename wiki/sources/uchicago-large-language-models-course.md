# UChicago Large Language Models Course

## Summary

An advanced University of Chicago course on LLMs covering three core frontiers: interpretability, alignment, and agents. Requires prior familiarity with transformers and language modeling.

## Key Takeaways

- Covers mechanistic interpretability (circuits, sparse autoencoders), alignment (scalable oversight, emergent misalignment), and agents (software engineering, scientific research, human simulation).
- Coursework emphasizes analyzing and interpreting empirical evidence from recent publications.
- Students complete a quarter-long research project.
- Compute provided by Modal.
- AI tools (Claude Code, ChatGPT) are allowed with disclosure.

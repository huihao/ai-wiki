# On Different Degrees of Smallness

## Summary
This chapter from Silvanus P. Thompson's "Calculus Made Easy" introduces the concept of infinitesimals through everyday analogies of relative minuteness. By comparing units of time, money, and precision, the text builds intuition for how quantities of different orders of smallness relate to each other, laying the conceptual groundwork for understanding derivatives and integrals in calculus.

## Key Takeaways
- Smallness is always relative: a minute is tiny compared to a week, but a second is even smaller relative to a minute
- If 1/60 is a "small fraction," then 1/3600 is a small fraction of the second order of smallness
- Higher-order smallness compounds: 1/1,000,000 of 1/1,000,000 = 1/1,000,000,000,000 (one billionth)
- The concept of relative minuteness is essential for understanding when small quantities can be safely ignored in calculations
- Even a golden sovereign is negligible relative to a millionaire's wealth, illustrating how scale changes perception
- The naming convention "second minutes" (seconds) literally means small quantities of the second order

# Critical Thinking during the age of AI

## Summary
Addy Osmani presents a framework for critical thinking in AI-augmented software development using the classic "Who, what, where, when, why, how" structure. The essay argues that as AI tools become more capable, human critical thinking becomes more essential, not less, and provides concrete techniques for evidence-based decision making, identifying root causes, and avoiding cognitive biases when working with AI-generated outputs.

## Key Takeaways
- Treat AI outputs like a junior developer's work: review, test, and verify everything before trusting it
- Use the "5 Whys" technique to drill past symptoms to root causes rather than applying quick fixes
- Confirmation bias and "plunging-in bias" are the biggest threats to sound technical decision-making
- Define the real problem before rushing to a solution; most failed decisions skip this step
- Context matters: a fix that works in a sandbox may fail in production
- Communicate with evidence and data, not opinions; cite metrics over claims
- Critical thinking is a continuous discipline, not a one-time checkbox

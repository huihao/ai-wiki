## Summary

Advanced LLM course at University of Chicago covering three frontier areas: mechanistic interpretability, alignment, and agents. Assumes transformer and language modeling background.

## Core Topics

### 1. Interpretability

- Circuit analysis and internal representations
- Geometry of model features
- Sparse autoencoders for feature probing
- Linear representations

### 2. Alignment

- Scalable oversight techniques
- Emergent misalignment concerns
- Ensuring intended model behavior

### 3. Agents

- Autonomous software engineering agents
- Scientific research agents
- Simulating human behavior
- Complementing human decision-making

## Coursework Structure

### Grading Breakdown

- **Quizzes**: 20% (assess reading understanding)
- **Roast or Toast**: 10% (critically analyze or extend papers)
- **Assignments**: 20% (three assignments throughout quarter)
- **Project**: 50% (quarter-long research project)

### Project Timeline

- Proposal Due: March 28
- Proposal Revision: April 4
- Weekly Blog Entries: April 10, 17, 24; May 1, 15
- First Draft: May 8
- Final Report: May 22

## Prerequisites

- **CMSC 25700/CMSC 35100**: Natural Language Processing
- **DATA 37712/CMSC 37712**: Foundations of Machine Learning II: Generative Models
- Expected: Transformer architecture understanding, LM training/analysis experience
- Preferred: Research experience

## Course Policies

### Collaboration

- Individual assignments: Collaborate but write independently, disclose collaboration
- Projects: Groups of 1-2 students (preferably 1)

### AI Tools

- Allowed with disclosure
- Encouraged heavy use (e.g., NeuriCo for projects)

### Late Days

- 3 late days total for assignments
- No late submissions for project work

## Compute Resources

- Modal has offered compute to each student
- Details available on Ed discussion platform

## Key Themes

- **Advanced Topics**: Frontier LLM research areas
- **Hands-On Research**: Quarter-long project on open problems
- **Critical Analysis**: Emphasis on analyzing incomplete empirical evidence
- **Practical Skills**: Assignments and real research experience

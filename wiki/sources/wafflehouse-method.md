# How to Stop Feeling Lost in Tech: The Wafflehouse Method

## Summary

Personal development framework for CS students and tech workers feeling lost. Uses a 48-hour "wafflehouse method" to go from vague life vision to concrete daily tasks through iterative goal refinement using a directed acyclic graph (DAG).

## Key Takeaways

- **Vision first**: Imagine yourself 5 years from now at your proudest moment — remove others' expectations.
- **48-hour process**: Take 2 days mid-week off, go alone to a meaningful spot.
- **Day 1**: Write everything — feelings, regrets, fears, hopes — until you have a vivid picture.
- **Day 2 at the wafflehouse**: Refine vision into 2-3 specific 5-year goals → interpolate to 3-year → 1-year goals.
- **DAG planning**: Create a directed acyclic graph with hundreds of steps from current state to goals, assuming every day is a bad day.
- **Unknot knot points**: Research unclear steps; some are 5 nodes mashed together, some are based on false priors.
- **Monthly → Weekly → Daily**: Cascade goals down to concrete actions starting NOW.
- **Weekly review**: Every Sunday, tend to the plan like a garden.
- **Key insight**: Once you have a personal plan, there's no one to blame but yourself — both liberating and stressful.
